# Balkan.works Investor Pitch Deck

## Purpose

Provide the narrative and evidence checklist for a Balkan.works investor presentation. This document is slide content guidance, not a public securities offering, financial guarantee or claim that every roadmap item and metric is already achieved. Before external use, validate all market facts, traction, legal statements and financial figures with current evidence and qualified advisers.

## Slide 1 — Balkan.works

**Connecting local economies through technology**

Balkan.works is a modular digital platform designed to connect people, local businesses and communities across the Balkans through trusted discovery, useful local information and governed intelligence.

## Slide 2 — Problem

Local discovery is fragmented. People can struggle to find reliable, current businesses and local offers across scattered search, social, map and directory channels. Small and medium businesses can struggle with visibility, customer acquisition and practical digital tools.

## Slide 3 — Market opportunity

The opportunity is the repeated local need for services, products and recommendations, alongside many businesses that need discoverability and growth tools. Quantify the relevant geography, customer segments and market size with dated, source-linked research before presenting this slide externally.

## Slide 4 — Solution

Balkan.works connects business profiles, local search, offers, reviews/trust, user preferences, analytics and future approval-based AI assistance through one ecosystem. The initial value proposition is simple: people find what they need nearby; businesses are easier to discover and can understand local interest.

## Slide 5 — Why now

Potential drivers include continued digital adoption, local search behavior, demand for reliable local information and practical AI-enabled business workflows. Present these as validated regional trends only with current evidence; avoid generic “AI transformation” claims without a user/business benefit.

## Slide 6 — Product progression

| Phase | Product focus |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Balkan Core: profiles, local discovery, offers and trust foundations |
| 2 | Growth platform: promotion workflow, analytics and value-led business tools |
| 3 | Transactional modules: reservations/marketplace only after operational readiness |
| 4 | Intelligence platform: grounded AI search, recommendations and approved assistance |

Each phase is gated by user/business value, data quality, security, legal and operational readiness.

## Slide 7 — Business model

Potential diversified revenue: optional business subscriptions, clearly labeled premium visibility/campaigns, future transaction commissions, governed AI tools and enterprise/partner solutions. Revenue lines are activated only when entitlements, pricing, support, compliance and customer value are proven.

## Slide 8 — Go-to-market

Start with one city and focused categories. Build the first 100 quality businesses through direct onboarding; create consumer value through current profiles, offers, local content, partnerships and careful referrals. Expand city-by-city after activation, retention and local network health demonstrate a repeatable model.

## Slide 9 — Competitive advantage

The intended advantage is a reinforcing local network:

`More active businesses → more useful local content → more successful discovery → more business outcomes → stronger network`

Defensibility comes from supply quality/freshness, trust operations, business workflows, local partnerships, governed data and responsible AI — not a feature claim alone. Competitive comparisons need sourced research.

## Slide 10 — Market expansion

`Pilot city → repeatable city playbook → country readiness → Balkan network → mature intelligence ecosystem`

Expansion includes language, category, policy, operations, support, partner and compliance readiness. Geography is not expanded before the current local loop works.

## Slide 11 — Key metrics

Use reproducible, dated metrics:

- Businesses: leads, activated/verified-eligible profiles, current offers, retention and paid conversion.
- Users: activated users, successful search/discovery, DAU/WAU/MAU where meaningful, retention and referrals.
- Platform: supply density, content freshness, support/moderation quality and successful connections.
- Financial: MRR/ARR when active, gross margin, CAC, LTV methodology, churn, burn/runway and transaction volume only when applicable.

Each chart states cohort, geography, date range, definition, source and known limitation.

## Slide 12 — Roadmap

| Horizon | Directional milestone |
| --- | --- |
| Year 1 | MVP, quality local supply, first active user base and validation evidence |
| Year 2 | Value-led monetization, selected business/growth tools and repeatable city learning |
| Year 3 | Mature mobile/partner ecosystem, selected transactional modules and grounded recommendations where ready |
| Year 4+ | Regional expansion and governed intelligence platform |

These are planning directions, not delivery promises. The detailed Execution/Module Roadmaps carry gates and dependencies.

## Slide 13 — Team

Initial ownership spans founder/CEO, product, technical leadership and growth/sales. Evolve with customer success, operations, quality/security, data/AI and regional leaders as actual constraints demand. Present current team names, roles, experience and hiring gaps accurately; do not list planned hires as current capacity.

## Slide 14 — Funding use

Potential use of capital includes product delivery, secure infrastructure, initial business acquisition/onboarding, user growth experiments, key hires and support/operations. Tie each allocation to milestones, budget, owner, time horizon and evidence required. Funding terms and legal materials are handled in the controlled Investor Data Room.

## Slide 15 — Vision

Balkan.works aims to become trusted digital infrastructure for local economies in the Balkans: a network of people, businesses, current information and responsible intelligence. It is not merely a static directory; it is a platform designed to make local discovery and business growth more useful over time.

## Closing statement

The future of local discovery, business growth and responsible AI-enabled regional commerce can start with Balkan.works — one useful city network at a time.

## Presentation rules

- Keep slides visually simple: one message and one evidence point per slide.
- Separate verified traction from target/forecast/vision explicitly.
- Cite market claims and label assumptions.
- Do not show personal data, secrets, private business metrics or security-sensitive architecture.
- Match the current repository/product status; do not imply deployed features that are only documented or planned.
