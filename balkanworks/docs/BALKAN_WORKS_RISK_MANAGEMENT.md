# Balkan.works Risk Management Plan

> Operating risk framework. It supports, but does not replace, qualified legal, security, financial or insurance advice.

## Purpose

Identify the risks that can materially undermine Balkan.works, notice their signals early and take proportionate action before they become incidents. The goal is not to eliminate all risk; it is to make risk visible, owned and manageable.

## Framework

Assess each risk by probability, impact and controllability. Classify it as **Low**, **Medium**, **High** or **Critical**. Every High or Critical risk needs an owner, an early-warning metric, a mitigation plan, a review date and an escalation path.

## Risk register

| Risk | Early signals | Primary mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Market | few active users, incomplete business profiles, weak return rate | user interviews, fast experiments, focus on a narrow local segment and behavior measurement |
| Product | many shipped features with little adoption | validate the need before build; track activation and usage per feature |
| Supply | empty categories, poor profiles, weak local coverage | founder-led onboarding, verification and profile-quality standards |
| Demand | low traffic, searches and repeat use | local content, SEO, partnerships and referrals |
| Competition | aggressive competitor spend or copied features | strengthen local data, trust, business outcomes and the network rather than feature parity |
| Technology | errors, slow responses or capacity limits | modular architecture, monitoring, testing, backups and progressive scaling |
| Security | suspicious access, privilege changes or exposure indicators | encryption, least privilege, audit logs, backups and incident response |
| Data quality | inaccurate contacts, stale offers or incorrect locations | verification, user correction/reporting, periodic reviews and quality scores |
| Trust | spam, fake reviews, poor profiles or rising reports | transparent policies, moderation, reputation controls and consistent appeals |
| Financial | short runway, uncontrolled spend or weak unit economics | burn/runway control, staged hiring and decisions tied to measurable outcomes |
| Team | slow delivery, unclear ownership or communication failures | clear roles, ownership culture, documented decisions and deliberate hiring |
| Legal | complaints, disputes or data-protection gaps | reviewed terms/policies, compliance processes and qualified local counsel |

## Priority matrix

**Critical:** insufficient user demand, insufficient quality business supply, poor data quality and insufficient capital.

**High:** security, technology scalability and credible competitive pressure.

**Medium:** organization, process maturity and automation gaps.

Priorities change with evidence. The register is reviewed weekly in the early stage and after every material incident or market expansion decision.

## Early-warning dashboard

- **Product:** active users, retention, searches and successful connections.
- **Business network:** new businesses, active/verified profiles, category coverage and profile quality.
- **Finance:** burn rate, runway, revenue and acquisition efficiency.
- **Trust:** reports, moderation volume, review quality and unresolved support issues.
- **Platform health:** availability, error rates, response times, backup status and security alerts.

## Crisis response

Use **detect → analyze → decide → act → review**. First protect people, data and service continuity; then assign an incident owner, scope the impact, contain the issue, record the decisions, communicate through approved channels where required, recover, and turn the outcome into a preventive action.

Security, legal and payment incidents follow the dedicated incident and notification procedures in the Security, DevOps and Legal frameworks.

## Founder weekly risk check

- Are people actively using the product and returning?
- Are businesses receiving visible value?
- Is growth healthy and measurable?
- Are we spending within the intended runway?
- What single failure could most slow or stop progress this week?

## Final principle

The greatest early-stage risk is building for too long without evidence that users and businesses want the result. Keep the launch focused, measure real behavior and correct course quickly.
