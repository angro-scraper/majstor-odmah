# Balkan.works Fundraising Strategy

> Strategic operating framework, not investment, legal or financial advice. Terms, valuations, securities compliance and financing decisions require qualified professional review.

## Purpose

Define how Balkan.works raises capital to accelerate validated progress without treating financing as a substitute for product-market evidence.

## Principle

Investment is a tool, not the goal. Raise capital to accelerate a working engine: a local business network, economic data asset and governed AI opportunity.

```text
Meaningful problem → large reachable market → differentiated solution
  → network evidence → data/AI potential → scalable company
```

## Funding stages and proof

| Stage | Primary purpose | Capital supports | Evidence to earn next stage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bootstrap / pre-seed | prove the core need | MVP, first businesses/users, market learning | people and businesses show real use/value |
| Seed | make growth repeatable | product, first team, sales and focused growth | repeatable city-level acquisition, retention and early revenue evidence |
| Series A | scale a proven model | more markets, team, infrastructure and AI capabilities | credible regional leadership path and healthy unit economics |
| Growth capital | extend established advantage | internationalization, selected acquisitions, advanced systems | durable growth, governance and disciplined deployment capacity |

## Use-of-funds discipline

Allocate each round against a specific milestone plan, owner and measurable outcome:

- **Product:** engineering, design, reliability and infrastructure.
- **Growth:** local content, marketing, partnerships and acquisition experiments.
- **Sales:** business acquisition, onboarding and customer success.
- **AI and data:** governed analytics, evaluated models and necessary infrastructure.
- **Team:** only roles that raise delivery speed, quality, revenue or operational control.

Avoid using capital to mask weak retention, thin supply, unproven channel economics or a lack of focus.

## Core investor metrics

- **Growth:** users, active businesses, local supply density and activity.
- **Product:** successful connections, activation, retention and engagement.
- **Business:** premium conversion, revenue, gross margin, LTV and retention.
- **Network:** city readiness, offer/content freshness and data quality.
- **Financial:** burn, runway, CAC, cash plan and milestone efficiency.

Metric definitions must be consistent, reproducible and sourceable in the investor data room.

## Pitch structure

1. Vision: digital infrastructure for the Balkan local economy.
2. Problem: fragmented discovery for people and expensive, weak digital reach for businesses.
3. Solution: one platform for discovery, trusted connection and business growth.
4. Market: reachable local business and consumer opportunity.
5. Product: user experience, business value and focused roadmap.
6. Traction: verified users, businesses, growth, retention and revenue evidence.
7. Business model: premium, promotion, transaction and future AI revenue layers.
8. Competition: local data, trust, operating depth and network defensibility.
9. AI opportunity: how governed data improves local outcomes.
10. Team and capital plan: why this team and this round can achieve the next proof point.

## Investor relationship strategy

Build relationships before a round. Maintain a permissioned record of contacts, relevant theses, conversations, feedback, next steps and commitments. Send concise monthly updates with progress, metrics, learnings, challenges, asks and upcoming milestones. Choose investors for aligned time horizon, market understanding, operating support and governance quality — not only capital.

## Common questions

| Question | Evidence-led answer |
| --- | --- |
| Why now? | digital local discovery and AI capabilities are changing expectations; show current local demand data |
| Why Balkan.works? | local operating knowledge, focused network-building and a governed data/trust strategy |
| Why not a global platform? | differentiate with verified local depth, relevant context and business outcomes, supported by actual results |
| How does it scale? | city-by-city replication after explicit readiness gates and documented playbooks |

## Fundraising risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Raising too early | validate product use, supply and retention first |
| Raising too much or unfocused capital | define a milestone-linked budget and operating cadence |
| Misaligned investor | diligence strategic fit, governance expectations and follow-on capacity |
| Metric inconsistency | maintain investor-ready definitions, source data and transparent caveats |

## Principles

- Raise enough for the next validated phase and reasonable runway.
- Protect long-term company governance and mission through informed legal/financial advice.
- Treat investors as long-term partners; communicate accurately, including risks and misses.
- Fund evidence-backed plans rather than feature lists.

## Final vision

Capital should accelerate the machine that already works. The strongest raise follows proof that Balkan.works can create durable value for people and businesses at local scale.
