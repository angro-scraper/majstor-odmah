# Balkan.works Team Structure

## Principle

Start with a small, high-ownership team that can validate the product quickly. Add specialist teams only after the platform has evidence of repeat user and business demand.

## Stage 1 — Foundation team (0–6 months, 3–6 people)

| Role | Core responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Founder / CEO | Vision, strategy, key partnerships and capital decisions |
| CTO / Technical Lead | Architecture, security, delivery standards and technical direction |
| Full-stack developer | Core API, web product and integrations |
| Product designer | UX, UI and the shared design system |
| Business development | Business onboarding, partners and sales |

One person may cover more than one role at this stage, but accountability stays explicit. Mobile delivery may be covered by the full-stack lead until native/mobile usage makes a dedicated hire necessary.

## Stage 2 — MVP growth team (6–18 months)

Trigger: the MVP shows repeat use, active businesses and a validated acquisition path.

- Backend engineers: API, services, database and scale.
- Mobile developer: iOS/Android experience and performance.
- QA engineer: test automation, release quality and regression control.
- Marketing lead: user growth, campaigns and brand.
- Community manager: local user feedback and city engagement.
- Sales: business acquisition, premium plans and partnerships.
- Product/DevOps responsibilities are added when roadmap or operational load requires them.

## Stage 3 — Regional expansion team (2–5 years)

Product adds a Head of Product, product managers and UX research. Engineering adds an engineering manager, backend, mobile, infrastructure and later AI teams. Business adds regional sales, partnerships and account management. Operations adds customer support, city managers and training. Product divisions evolve around Balkan Core, Business Platform, Marketplace and AI Platform; feature teams own outcomes end-to-end.

## Stage 4 — AI platform company (5+ years)

An AI division (ML engineers, data engineers and AI product management), data division (pipelines, analytics and intelligence) and infrastructure/security/reliability division are created only when the platform has the data, product demand and operating maturity to support them. Country managers, city teams and local operations grow only after the city model is repeatable.

## Hiring order

1. CTO / technical leadership
2. Full-stack delivery capacity
3. Business development
4. Product design
5. Growth specialist
6. QA, mobile, backend and operations based on demonstrated bottlenecks

## Operating cadence

- Weekly product review: what is shipping, learning and blocked.
- Weekly business review: business onboarding, partners, revenue and campaigns.
- Monthly strategy review: roadmap, capital, partner pipeline and priorities.

## Metrics

- Product: active users, activation, retention and support resolution.
- Business: activated businesses, offer activity, revenue and retention.
- Engineering: reliability, delivery lead time, security findings and operational load.

## Culture

Ownership, speed with discipline, customer value, documented decisions and measurable learning. Avoid large marketing teams, broad management layers or unplanned engineering hiring before the product evidence justifies them.

## Organization map

`CEO → CTO / Product / Business / Operations → Engineering → AI + Data + Infrastructure`

The structure stays problem-led: hire to remove a validated delivery, quality, local-operations or customer-value constraint — never for title inflation.
