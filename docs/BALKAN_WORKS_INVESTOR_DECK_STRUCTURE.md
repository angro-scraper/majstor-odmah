# Balkan.works Investor Deck Structure

## Purpose

Define a 20-slide presentation structure for qualified investors, strategic partners, enterprises and institutions. It communicates vision, market, technology and commercial potential while distinguishing implemented evidence from roadmap direction.

This is presentation structure, not a public offering, investment recommendation or claim that future modules/market figures are already live. Validate every external market, traction, legal and financial statement before presentation.

## Slide 1 — Title

**Balkan.works**  
**One App. One Network. One Balkan.**

A modular digital ecosystem connecting people, businesses, services, commerce and business tools through a shared trusted platform.

## Slide 2 — The problem

Local discovery and business operations are fragmented. Users navigate multiple channels for local needs; businesses may use disconnected marketing, operational and customer tools. Present specific customer research and local examples, not broad unsupported claims.

## Slide 3 — The opportunity

The regional opportunity is shared local needs, a large small-business base, connected markets/languages and ongoing digitalization. Add dated, sourced market data relevant to the pilot/countries; separate addressable market hypothesis from measured demand.

## Slide 4 — The solution

Balkan.works brings together local discovery, offers and trust for users, and business profiles, visibility and tools for businesses. The initial promise: users discover relevant local value; businesses become easier to find and understand local customer interest.

## Slide 5 — Super-app model

```text
Balkan.works Core
├── Offers / Digital Flyers
├── Save Food (future module)
├── Opsnestone (future business-tools integration)
├── Stock Radar (future intelligence module)
├── Marketplace (future module)
└── AI layer
```

All modules use Balkan ID, permissions, data/API contracts and feature flags. Label current, pilot and planned modules visually on the actual deck.

## Slide 6 — Why now

Potential timing: small-business digitization, local/mobile discovery behavior, practical AI assistance and demand for trusted information. Cite sources and tie the claim to a concrete product advantage; do not use “AI revolution” as a standalone argument.

## Slide 7 — Product ecosystem

- **User layer:** Balkan ID, discovery, offers, favorites/future rewards.
- **Business layer:** profile, offers/flyers, future Opsnestone capabilities.
- **Intelligence layer:** analytics, governed AI search/recommendations and future Stock Radar.

Explain that shared identity, trust and data boundaries are what make the ecosystem coherent.

## Slide 8 — First product / MVP

Core MVP: users, business profiles, offers/digital flyer foundation, local search, moderation and basic analytics. Show a real demo, flow or evidence if available; otherwise label as current build scope. Marketplace, wallet and advanced AI remain non-goals until readiness gates.

## Slide 9 — Network effect

`More active users ↔ more active businesses → more current offers/local content → more successful connections → stronger network`

Show the city-level supply/demand loop and why profile quality/freshness/trust matter more than raw listings.

## Slide 10 — Business model

Potential revenue lines: business SaaS, Opsnestone tools when ready, optional promotion/Digital Flyers, later transaction commissions, premium AI tools and partner solutions. Explain which revenue is current versus future and the customer value/operational prerequisites behind each.

## Slide 11 — Competitive advantage

Balkan.works combines a local network, business enablement, trust operations, shared ecosystem contracts and responsible intelligence. The moat must be presented as a thesis to prove through retention, supply density, business outcomes, partnerships and data quality — not as a completed fact.

## Slide 12 — Technology advantage

Modular monolith foundation, API-first contracts, PostgreSQL data model, shared UI, feature flags, observability/security boundaries and AI gateway readiness. Emphasize that scaling follows evidence; architecture documentation is not a claim that all infrastructure has been deployed.

## Slide 13 — AI strategy

User AI: grounded search, recommendations and assistance. Business AI: approved content drafting, analytics support and campaigns. Future agents require scoped permissions, confirmation, audit trails, evaluation, privacy and human override.

## Slide 14 — Go-to-market

`One pilot city → prove local loop → more cities → country readiness → regional expansion → diaspora opportunities`

Initial geography, language and city order are chosen by evidence: business density, partner access, digital readiness, operations and local retention.

## Slide 15 — Initial users and businesses

Target segments may include local consumers, families, professionals and new residents, alongside restaurants, shops, local services and small businesses. Explain the first-business and first-user playbooks; include actual cohort evidence where available.

## Slide 16 — Partnerships

Potential partners include business associations, local communities/cities, banks, telecoms, retail/food networks, universities and local media. Each partnership needs mutual value, data/permission boundaries, attribution, integration readiness and owner — not just a logo slide.

## Slide 17 — Traction plan

First-year direction: one city, hundreds of active quality businesses, thousands of activated users and initial paid-value evidence. Show actual traction, if any, separately from targets. Key proof: successful connections, retention, supply freshness, business outcomes and support/trust health.

## Slide 18 — Financial model

Show phased revenue/expense logic: early validation, optional paid value, repeatable business growth and later selected transactions/intelligence. Use MRR/ARR, margin, CAC/LTV methodology, churn and runway only with dated definitions and evidence. Forecasts state assumptions/scenarios.

## Slide 19 — Funding use

Funding can support product delivery, pilot/city growth, key team hires, support/operations, secure infrastructure and governed AI/data capability. Tie each use category to milestones, budget, owner and expected evidence; funding terms belong in controlled legal/data-room processes.

## Slide 20 — Long-term vision

Balkan.works aims to become a trusted digital operating layer for local economies in the Balkans: people discover and act, businesses grow and regional knowledge becomes more useful through responsible technology.

## Investor message

**We are not building another isolated application. We are building a network that can connect the region, starting with one useful city at a time.**

## Deck construction rules

- Keep one message/evidence point per slide; use real product/demo/cohort evidence where possible.
- Label each capability **live**, **pilot**, **in development** or **planned**.
- Cite market numbers and avoid confidential customer/business data.
- Keep legal/financial/AI claims qualified and evidence-backed.
- Use the Pitch Deck, Investment Pitch and Investor Data Room as supporting materials, not duplicated sources of truth.
