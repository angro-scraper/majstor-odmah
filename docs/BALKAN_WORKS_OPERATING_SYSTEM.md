# Balkan.works Operating System

## Purpose

Define how Balkan.works operates each day, week, month and quarter so the company can grow without losing focus, quality, speed or accountability.

## Operating principle

`Measure → learn → decide → execute → measure again`

The operating system converts evidence into action. It avoids activity for its own sake and makes ownership, risks and outcomes visible.

## Company pillars

| Pillar | Core question | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Product | Are we solving a real user/business problem? | Useful, understandable and trusted product flows |
| Technology | Is the system reliable and safe to change? | Stable, observable modular infrastructure |
| Growth | Are the right users discovering and returning? | Activated, retained local demand |
| Sales | Are businesses seeing and sustaining value? | Active quality supply and evidence-led paid value |
| Operations | Can we support, moderate and recover reliably? | Healthy service and trusted execution |
| Data | What does evidence say we should do next? | Clear decisions and learning loops |

## Daily operating system

Review a focused health view each day:

- **Product:** core errors, user friction, support signals and meaningful activity.
- **Growth:** activated users, acquisition source quality, content/campaign changes.
- **Sales:** qualified leads, conversations, demos, active business profiles and first-value blockers.
- **Technology:** availability, latency/errors, deployment changes, security/queue/database alerts.
- **Operations:** moderation queue, priority tickets, incidents and owner escalations.

The daily check ends with owners and next actions, not a long status report.

## Weekly operating rhythm

| Day/ritual | Focus | Required output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monday planning | What matters most this week? | Prioritized outcomes, owners, evidence target, dependencies and risks |
| Wednesday product review | Are we building the right thing? | Feedback/usage review, scope adjustment or experiment decision |
| Friday growth/business review | Are the local loops improving? | User/business funnel, channel, retention and learning review |
| Weekly delivery/quality review | Is work safe to release? | Demo, test/monitoring status, known risks and release decision |

Meeting cadence should be adapted to team size; written updates replace meetings that do not change a decision.

## Monthly business review

Review actuals against plan: active users/businesses, successful connections, search/offer quality, retention cohorts, business activation, revenue/margin where active, burn/runway, support/moderation, reliability/security and key risks. Decide what to continue, stop, fix, defer or accelerate. Record evidence, decision owner and revisit date.

## Quarterly strategy review

Reassess market/pilot-city conditions, product-market evidence, channels/partnerships, team capacity, technology/security debt, legal/compliance needs, funding/budget and expansion readiness. Update the roadmap only through a documented decision; do not shift direction silently because of a new idea or competitor headline.

## Decision system

Every material decision answers:

1. What problem are we solving?
2. What evidence do we have?
3. How will success, harm or failure be measured?
4. What is the cost/risk if we are wrong?
5. Who owns the decision and when is it reviewed?

Use an impact-confidence-effort view, expanded with risk/dependency/readiness. High impact and low effort can be prioritized only when security, privacy, operational and support requirements are met.

## Company dashboard

The executive view stays small:

- Growth: activated users, active businesses, acquisition/activation quality.
- Product: successful connections, search success, retention and key friction.
- Revenue: recognized MRR/ARR, gross margin, conversion and runway only when relevant/live.
- Quality: support satisfaction, moderation health, incidents, reliability and data-quality signals.

Metric definitions, source, dates, city/cohort and limitations are visible. Registrations, traffic and listing counts are never used alone as success proof.

## Culture and communication

- Customers and businesses are sources of truth; data and conversations complement each other.
- Learning speed matters, but quality/security/trust are non-negotiable.
- Simplicity beats unowned complexity.
- Everyone owns an outcome, not only a task.
- Internal communication is concise, factual and actionable.

Problem reports use: **problem X; evidence/cause Y; proposed next action Z; owner/timeframe.** Escalate uncertainty and bad news early.

## Documentation and experimentation

Document material decisions, processes, experiments, results, incidents and operating metrics in the repository/approved systems. Each experiment includes a hypothesis, target cohort/city, change, primary and guardrail metric, duration, owner, result and ship/iterate/stop decision. Learning persists when people change roles.

## Scale principle

Do not scale chaos. First standardize a successful repeatable process, document it, assign ownership, instrument it and only then automate or expand it. The organization evolves from startup to growth company to regional platform through proven operating loops, not through titles or hierarchy.

## Final principle

Balkan.works wins by learning continuously, adapting quickly and building long-term value. The objective is not to grow fastest for one moment; it is to keep growing responsibly for years.
