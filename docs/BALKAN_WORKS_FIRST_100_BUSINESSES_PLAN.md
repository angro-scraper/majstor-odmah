# Balkan.works First 100 Businesses Plan

## Purpose

The first 100 businesses are not merely accounts. They are the initial local supply, credibility and foundation of the Balkan.works network effect. The goal is 100 active, accurate businesses in one defined city or region — not 100 incomplete listings.

## Success definition

A business counts as active when its profile meets the local quality threshold: at least 80% required profile completion, confirmed contact details, at least one authorized image, valid category/location, owner access to the platform and a completed verification level appropriate to the available evidence. Verification labels must remain precise; no business is called “verified” until the documented review is complete.

**North Star:** active, quality-checked local businesses with current information.

## Market focus

Start with one city or tightly defined region. Do not expand the geography until active supply, business support and consumer discovery show that the local loop works.

| Tier | Starting categories | Rationale to validate locally |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Restaurants, cafés, auto services, hair/beauty, plumbers, electricians | Frequent local needs and visible business/customer interaction |
| 2 | Dentists/private practices, language schools, fitness, accounting services | Trust-sensitive or repeat-use categories requiring accurate policy review |
| 3 | Tourism, specialist shops, local producers | Expand once local demand/supply quality is proven |

Category selection follows pilot-city research, legal/moderation requirements and actual business willingness — not a fixed universal list.

## Acquisition model

`Direct outreach + referrals + qualified partnerships + local presence → profile setup → verification → activation → retained local supply`

The initial offer is a free profile, a clear explanation of the platform and voluntary help entering accurate data. Do not promise traffic, revenue, premium placement or unbuilt functionality.

## Phase 1 — First 25 businesses

**Goal:** learn the process manually.

The founder/team directly contacts carefully selected local businesses by phone, email, local visit or professional network where appropriate. Use each conversation to learn discovery needs, correct profile data, trust concerns and onboarding friction.

**Target:** 25 active quality profiles, with documented reasons for success, rejection or dropout.

## Phase 2 — 25 to 50 businesses

**Goal:** prove a repeatable onboarding motion.

Turn what worked into a short onboarding guide, appropriate follow-up sequences, landing content and a basic referral flow. A business referral may receive a future eligible visibility benefit or trial only after pricing/entitlement rules exist; avoid incentives that create fake, inactive or misleading profiles.

**Target:** 50 active businesses and a measured conversion/activation funnel.

## Phase 3 — 50 to 100 businesses

**Goal:** create the first meaningful local network effect.

Focus on categories that show current profiles, customer interest and business retention. Add local events, community/association partners and introductions from successful businesses. Protect quality: stale, duplicate, inaccurate or unsupported profiles reduce the value of the network.

**Target:** 100 active quality businesses with named owner/support coverage and evidence that the local discovery loop is useful.

## Outreach script

Use a short, honest invitation:

> Zdravo, pokrećemo Balkan.works — platformu koja pomaže lokalnim firmama da budu lakše pronađene u svom gradu. Želeli bismo da vašu firmu uključimo među prve kvalitetne profile bez početnog troška i pomognemo pri unosu podataka. Da li imate deset minuta za kratko predstavljanje?

Identify the sender, honor communication preferences and record consent/opt-out where required. Adjust the message to the business's category and never imply endorsement or verification before it occurs.

## Follow-up and onboarding

`Contact → response → discovery/demo → profile setup → quality review → verification path → activation → first value follow-up`

Each business receives a clear owner, next step and support path. Follow up on profile completion, first customer-interest signal, offer freshness where relevant and factual feedback. Avoid repetitive outreach after an opt-out or lack of consent.

## Onboarding checklist

- Correct business name and category
- Clear description and contact details
- Accurate address/location and opening hours
- Authorized logo/photo(s)
- Website/social links when available and correct
- Owner/business membership
- Appropriate verification status/evidence
- First current offer or call-to-action where suitable

## KPI dashboard

| Area | Measure |
| --- | --- |
| Outreach | Qualified contacts, responses, discovery conversations, demos and opt-outs |
| Funnel | Registrations, profile completion, verification completion, activation and time to first value |
| Quality | Accurate/complete profiles, media authorization, current offers, duplicate/stale rate and business satisfaction |
| Network | Active verified-eligible businesses, category/city density, consumer discovery success and business retention |

A directional first funnel hypothesis may be 100 qualified contacts → 40 conversations → 25 registrations → 20 active profiles. Treat it as a learning benchmark, not a quota: update it with city/category evidence and prioritize profile quality over volume.

## Partnerships

Potential partners include chambers, local associations, coworking spaces, business clubs and community organizations. Partner relationships need a clear shared value proposition, owner, attribution, privacy/data boundary and activation metric. A partner is valuable when it delivers retained, quality local supply — not just introductions.

## Common objections

| Objection | Honest response |
| --- | --- |
| “Nemamo vremena.” | “Počinjemo samo sa osnovnim, tačnim podacima i pomažemo vam pri unosu; vi odobravate profil.” |
| “Već imamo društvene mreže.” | “Balkan.works ih ne menja; dodaje strukturiran lokalni kanal gde ljudi mogu pronaći firmu i aktualne informacije.” |
| “Koliko košta?” | “Početni profil je besplatan. Plaćene funkcije, kada postoje, biće jasne i opcione — ne obećavamo ih unapred.” |

## Hiring trigger

When 100 active businesses are supported reliably or the team can consistently activate roughly 20 quality businesses per month, evaluate a business-development/customer-success hire. The trigger also requires a repeatable process, clear role ownership and budget; volume alone is not enough.

## Final principle

The first 100 businesses come from direct work, conversations and quality discipline. They establish the trust and local usefulness that later marketing and automation can amplify.
