# Balkan.works First 180 Days Plan

## Purpose

Define the first operating period for Balkan.works: deliver a functional MVP, activate initial local businesses and users, and obtain evidence that the local network creates value before scaling.

## Operating principle

Every period has an objective, accountable execution, measurable evidence and a decision. The plan is outcome-led, not a promise that all future modules will ship in 180 days.

`Days 1–30: Foundation → 31–60: Core build → 61–90: Pilot/soft launch → 91–120: Validation → 121–150: Repeatable growth → 151–180: Scale readiness`

## Days 1–30 — Foundation

| Week | Focus | Evidence of completion |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Product/company operating setup | Vision, MVP priority, decision owners, working documentation and delivery cadence agreed |
| 2 | Product preparation | MVP scope, primary consumer/business/admin flows, design-system baseline and acceptance criteria ready |
| 3 | Technical foundation | Repository, modular API/app structure, database plan, environment approach and security/testing baseline prepared |
| 4 | Internal vertical slice | A demonstrable internal flow for authentication, core models and initial interface; gaps are recorded honestly |

The output is a controlled foundation, not a public launch. Production credentials, external integrations and legal/payment claims remain out of scope until separately verified.

## Days 31–60 — Core product build

| Week | Focus | Acceptance evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 5–6 | Balkan ID, user profiles and business profiles | Validated role-aware flows; business profile lifecycle and ownership rules documented/tested |
| 7 | Categories, locations and discovery | Country/city taxonomy, filters and useful local search behavior with test data |
| 8 | Offers and public business experience | Authorized offer creation, valid visibility/expiry behavior and business profile display |

Prioritize end-to-end MVP quality over adding marketplace, wallet, autonomous AI or unrelated modules.

## Days 61–90 — Pilot and soft launch

| Week | Focus | Evidence of completion |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 9 | Quality and launch checks | Relevant QA/API/security/performance checks, release risks, monitoring and rollback readiness reviewed |
| 10 | Pilot supply preparation | First businesses, accurate local profiles/offers, onboarding/support path and pilot city scope prepared |
| 11 | Controlled soft launch | Invited users/businesses can complete core discovery and offer flows with support coverage |
| 12 | Evidence review | Activation, search success, offer activity, support issues and feedback synthesized into prioritized fixes |

Launch only with accurate content, moderation ownership and a way to respond to user/business issues. A download or signup count alone is not validation.

## Days 91–120 — Market validation

Focus on proving the first city loop. Business work targets qualified outreach, profile completion, current offers and measurable first value. Consumer work targets useful local content, discovery, referrals and community feedback. Product work resolves the strongest evidence-based blockers in search, offer display, onboarding and trust.

Decision questions: Are businesses staying active? Do users find useful local results? Does activity lead to a business-visible outcome? Which city/category conditions are required for this to repeat?

## Days 121–150 — Repeatable growth

Broaden categories only where local quality remains high. Test value-led premium interest rather than forcing monetization. Run attributable content, partner and local-campaign experiments; improve analytics definitions and cohort reporting. Retain the guardrails from Growth Loops: do not buy non-retained users, create stale SEO content or expand geography before the first local loop works.

## Days 151–180 — Scale preparation

Prepare decisions rather than prematurely launching every module:

- **Product:** validated mobile roadmap, premium capability candidates and next discovery improvements.
- **Business:** repeatable sales/onboarding playbook, packages only where value exists and partner pipeline.
- **Technology:** monitoring, backup/restore evidence, deployment automation, capacity/risk review and prioritized technical debt.
- **Operations:** support ownership, moderation process, city launch checklist and metric review cadence.

AI, payments, wallet and external-platform integrations remain planned work until their data, security, legal, provider and product readiness gates are met.

## Metrics and review cadence

| Dimension | Measure |
| --- | --- |
| Product | activated users, successful searches, offer views/saves/actions, retention, reliability/support issues |
| Business | qualified leads, activated profiles, verification completion, active/current offers, retained supply, business feedback |
| Growth | attributable acquisition, referral/organic share, activation cost, local network density |
| Operations | support response/resolution, moderation backlog, incident/release health |

Review weekly for execution blockers and monthly for cohort/city decisions. Every metric has an owner, definition, source, period and known limitation.

## Day-180 success criteria

At day 180, Balkan.works should have a stable, tested MVP scope; active local users; a credible network of current local business profiles/offers; support and operational feedback; clear metrics; and an evidence-based plan for the next city or next product layer. Targets are set per pilot-city capacity and documented as assumptions, not invented outcomes.

## What not to do

Do not introduce a complex marketplace, wallet/payments, broad autonomous AI, many unrelated modules, geographic expansion without local retention or expensive campaigns without attributable measurement. A focused, trusted first network is more valuable than a wide but inactive product.

## Final vision

The first 180 days prove more than an application: they establish whether Balkan.works can create a durable local platform loop that is ready to repeat responsibly.
