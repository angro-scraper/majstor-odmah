# Balkan.works Sales Playbook

## Purpose

Define a repeatable, ethical commercial process for finding, activating, retaining and — after value is demonstrated — converting local businesses to paid Balkan.works plans.

## Principle

Do not sell a listing. Sell measurable business value: discoverability, qualified customer interest, useful insight and a simpler digital presence. A business that is not a fit should not be pressured into a plan.

## Funnel

`Lead generation → first contact → discovery → relevant demo → profile creation → activation → outcome review → value-led premium conversion → retention`

The sales funnel connects directly to the Business Onboarding System. A signup alone is not a win; a usable, active business profile with a first value moment is.

## Ideal customer profile

Initial focus is on local, customer-facing businesses such as restaurants, shops, services, salons and tradespeople. The best early partners want new customers, offer a quality service, have a defined local area and can benefit from a maintained digital profile and current offers.

Qualification should record city, category, local coverage, decision-maker, current discovery channels, stated need, profile readiness and next agreed action. Do not collect unnecessary personal information.

## Lead sources

| Source | Use | Quality check |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Local research | Build city/category coverage deliberately | Public business relevance and category fit |
| Direct outreach | Phone, email or in-person local contact | Consent, correct contact and clear purpose |
| Partnerships | Associations, communities and institutions | Trusted distribution and shared measurable value |
| Organic/referral | Users, businesses and local discovery | Attribution, activation and retention quality |

## Sales process

### 1. Prepare

Research the public business presence, category, location, existing digital channels and likely local need. Enter only factual information in the CRM; do not fabricate a problem or promise an outcome.

### 2. Make first contact

The goal is attention and permission for a relevant conversation, not an immediate sale. A simple message is: “Pomozite ljudima da vas lakše pronađu u vašem gradu.” State who is contacting the business, why, and how to opt out of future outreach.

### 3. Run discovery

Understand how customers find the business today, which channels create value, what visibility challenge exists, how results are measured and whether the business has time to keep a profile current. Listen before proposing a product. Record consented notes and the mutually agreed next step.

### 4. Give a relevant demo

Show the business profile, local discovery path, offers and plain-language analytics using realistic product capability only. Do not claim live traffic, AI results, integrations, revenue or guarantees that the platform cannot evidence.

### 5. Activate

Create the business profile, confirm essential contact/location data, complete the quality checklist, choose an appropriate verification route and publish the first valid offer when ready. The handoff to onboarding is explicit: owner, next action and support channel are known.

### 6. Follow up on outcomes

Review profile views, customer interactions, offer activity, profile quality and business feedback. Use factual evidence to recommend the next action: improve the profile, refresh an offer, complete verification or pause when the platform is not yet valuable for the business.

## Positioning and objection handling

| Situation | Responsible response |
| --- | --- |
| “We already use Google/Facebook.” | “Balkan.works complements existing channels by helping people find relevant local businesses and offers.” |
| “We do not have time.” | “Start with the essential profile. The goal is to reduce repeated effort, not create another complicated tool.” |
| “How do we know it works?” | “We show the available evidence: profile/offer activity and customer interest. We do not guarantee sales.” |
| “We do not want a paid plan.” | “The basic profile can establish value first. Premium is optional and only makes sense when a specific capability helps.” |

## Packages and conversion

| Plan | Role | Potential entitlement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Free | Enter and activate the local network | Core profile and constrained offer capability |
| Premium | Improve demonstrated local visibility | Additional visibility, promotion and analytics where released |
| Business Pro | Serve mature operational needs | Future marketing, AI and business-tool capabilities behind feature flags |

Never make unreleased capabilities, payment terms or AI automation part of a sales promise. Premium prompts are appropriate only after the business sees measurable platform value and has clear pricing, entitlements and cancellation information.

## CRM, handoffs and data

Use a permissioned CRM or the future Opsnestone layer for lead source, stage, owner, agreed next action, product fit and outcomes. Apply tenant isolation, access controls, retention rules and audit logs. Outreach automation must respect opt-out, frequency caps and applicable communication rules.

## Metrics

Measure leads by source, contact rate, qualified discovery rate, demo rate, signups, profile completion, time to activation, first-value rate, active businesses, offer freshness, paid conversion, MRR/ARR when billing is released, retention and churn. Assess quality by city and category; avoid optimizing for lead volume that does not create active local supply.

## Team model

At the earliest stage, the founder and a sales owner validate the motion. With repeatable activation, add sales representatives and account/customer-success ownership. At scale, organize regional managers, sales representatives and customer success around local market health, not just closed contracts.

## Daily operating cadence

- Morning: review qualified leads, prepare contacts and prioritize follow-ups with agreed actions.
- Day: conduct discovery conversations and accurate product demonstrations.
- End of day: update CRM outcomes, hand off activation tasks and inspect funnel quality.
- Weekly: review conversion, activation, retention, objection patterns and customer feedback; improve the process.

## Final vision

Sales at Balkan.works is not a listing quota. It is the disciplined creation of a network of active local partners who can see value and choose to grow with the platform.
