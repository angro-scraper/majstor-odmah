# Balkan.works 10-Year Vision

## North star

Build the digital operating layer for everyday local life and business in the Balkans: people discover what they need, businesses run and grow their operations, cities support local networks and AI helps people make better decisions under human control.

This is a direction, not a guarantee or a fixed delivery schedule. Progress to each stage depends on verified user value, sustainable operations, security, legal readiness and local trust.

## Year 1 — Prove the local model

One city, an active base of businesses, useful offers and a reliable Core: Balkan ID, business profiles, discovery and Digital Flyers. The objective is evidence of repeat consumer value and measurable business outcomes, not geographic coverage.

## Years 2–3 — Repeatable city network

Replicate the city playbook in selected locations; introduce Save Food, consent-aware recommendations, rewards and stronger analytics where they improve daily use. Expand only where localization, local operations and support are ready.

## Years 4–5 — Business ecosystem

Develop Opsnestone as a dependable business operating layer, with opt-in integrations, governed analytics, practical business AI and an appropriate marketplace model. Small and medium businesses should be able to see clear operational and growth value.

## Years 6–7 — Intelligence platform

Use trusted, permissioned data to improve recommendations, analytics and controlled agents: personal assistance, business copilot and finance/insight support. AI remains grounded, explainable where needed and unable to take high-impact action without approval.

## Years 8–10 — Regional infrastructure

Connect a mature, localized network across the region and diaspora. Consumers may use the ecosystem for discovery, commerce, services, events, travel and savings. Businesses may use it for sales, marketing, operations, financial workflows and analytics. Financial, health and other regulated modules launch only with qualified providers and jurisdiction-specific controls.

## Expansion horizon

The intended sequence is Serbia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Montenegro, Slovenia, North Macedonia and diaspora communities. Each country and city earns its place through a working local network, language/currency support, compliant operations and a capable local team.

## Durable advantage

The defensibility is not code or AI in isolation. It is the combination of a trusted local network, useful data governed responsibly, business outcomes, regional operating knowledge and a platform users and partners choose to return to.

## Success test

The vision is working when users find it indispensable for local life, businesses can demonstrate value from participation and partners regard it as a trustworthy regional channel — while the platform continues to protect people, data and local communities.
