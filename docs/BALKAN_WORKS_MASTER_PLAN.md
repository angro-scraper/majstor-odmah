# Balkan.works Master Plan

## Executive summary

Balkan.works is a modular digital platform that connects people, businesses and local economies across the Balkans. The ambition is to build a trusted local network where people discover relevant services and businesses grow through digital presence, useful tools, governed data and later responsible AI.

## The big idea

Local information is often scattered, incomplete and hard to trust. People need a clearer way to discover relevant local businesses; businesses need discoverability, customer interest and practical tools. Balkan.works connects:

`People + businesses + trust + data + accountable intelligence`

The initial product must solve local discovery well before the platform attempts to solve every local-economy problem.

## Company evolution

| Phase | Platform role | Core capabilities | Evidence required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Local discovery | Best local place to find eligible businesses | Search, profiles, categories, locations, offers, reviews/trust | Useful discovery, active supply and retention |
| 2. Business growth | Help businesses create ongoing value | Value-led premium visibility, analytics, promotions and marketing tools | Business first value, retained supply and willingness to pay |
| 3. Transaction layer | Complete selected local needs | Reservations, purchases, communication and payment-enabled flows | Operational, legal, payment/security and support readiness |
| 4. AI economy | Governed intelligence layer | AI search, recommendations, assistants and permissioned agents | Quality data, evaluation, privacy, human control and measurable lift |

## Product strategy

### For people

Deliver fast local discovery, clear information, trustworthy signals, relevant recommendations and simple actions. Registration enables meaningful additional value such as saving/following/personalization; it is not an obstacle before a visitor sees the platform's usefulness.

### For businesses

Deliver a credible digital profile, local visibility, current offers, factual customer-interest insight and later tools that help growth. Business onboarding moves from profile completion and verification to first value and only then optional paid value.

## Data and intelligence strategy

The long-term asset is governed understanding of local economies: current business/service/location data, trust signals, permissioned user intent and quality interaction outcomes. Data is collected minimally and transparently, protected by tenant isolation and access controls, and translated first into better search/analytics — not speculative AI complexity.

AI progresses through grounded search, transparent recommendations, approval-based user/business assistance and eventually permissioned agents. It uses the AI Gateway, retrieval/knowledge layer, evaluation, auditability and human confirmation for consequential actions.

## Competitive advantage

The intended moat is a locally dense, trusted, current network:

`More active businesses → more useful local content → more successful connections → more business outcomes → stronger network`

Trust operations, verified/accurate information, business workflows, local partners, quality data and responsible intelligence reinforce one another. No single feature, model or marketing channel is the moat.

## Business model

Potential revenue diversifies across optional business subscriptions, transparently labeled premium visibility/campaigns, later transaction commissions, governed AI tools and enterprise/partner solutions. Revenue begins only where customer value, entitlements, pricing, support, security, legal and operational controls are ready. The financial model tracks revenue quality, margin, CAC/LTV methodology, burn/runway and retention rather than promises.

## Go-to-market

Start with one city. Acquire the first 100 quality businesses directly, then activate consumers through useful supply, local content/SEO, partnerships, community and eligible referrals. Validate the local loop before expanding categories, cities or countries.

Directional milestones such as 100 businesses and 10,000 users are evidence targets, not success in isolation. The decision gate is active quality supply, successful user discovery, retained participation, support/moderation capacity and viable acquisition economics.

## Execution roadmap

| Horizon | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| First 3 months | MVP vertical flows, internal quality and first local business preparation |
| First year | Pilot/public learning, first city health, retention and initial paid-value evidence |
| Year 2 | Repeatable city expansion, premium/business growth tools and stronger operating base |
| Year 3 | Selected transaction modules, grounded AI capabilities and regional expansion evidence |
| Year 5 direction | Trusted regional local-economy infrastructure, subject to market/operations evidence |

Exact delivery dates and scale targets are planning assumptions reviewed through the Execution Roadmap, First 180 Days Plan and KPI Dashboard.

## Company structure

The founder team owns vision, technical/product direction and growth/sales learning. As constraints appear, add focused product/engineering, customer success, sales/partnerships, operations/security and later data/AI capacity. Avoid hierarchy before there is a real ownership problem to solve.

The operating system is: `Measure → learn → decide → execute → measure again`. Material decisions have a problem statement, evidence, owner, success/guardrail metric, risk and review date.

## Core metrics

**North Star:** Successful Connections between people and relevant local businesses.

Track activated/retained users, active/verified-eligible businesses, profile/offer freshness, search success, contacts/qualified business outcomes, support/trust health, recognized revenue/margin when active and local network density. Avoid registrations, traffic and listing counts without activation/retention context.

## Investment story

Balkan.works may become regional digital infrastructure by combining local supply, network effects, trust, business enablement, governed data and AI. The investor case is based on evidence: city-level product/market learning, business retention, customer outcomes, disciplined unit economics, technical/security readiness and a credible expansion playbook. The Investor Data Room and Pitch Deck separate current facts from future plans.

## Why this can win

The platform is designed to win through local knowledge, repeatable city operations, trusted data, business relationships and AI that improves real outcomes rather than generating noise. It does not seek to imitate every competitor; it seeks to make a local network more useful each time someone participates.

## Final vision

Balkan.works becomes the place where people find what they need nearby, businesses grow and local economies become easier to understand and participate in digitally.

**We are not building only an application. We are building infrastructure that connects the Balkans.**
