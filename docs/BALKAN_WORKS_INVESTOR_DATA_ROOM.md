# Balkan.works Investor Data Room

## Purpose

Define a controlled structure for materials shared with qualified investors, strategic partners and advisers. The data room explains the Balkan.works strategy, product and operating evidence while protecting confidential, personal and commercially sensitive information.

It is an internal preparation guide, not an offer to sell securities, a guarantee of funding or a claim that every listed artifact already exists. Financial, corporate and legal materials require qualified professional review before external sharing.

## Company overview

**Company:** Balkan.works  
**Category:** Modular digital ecosystem platform  
**Vision:** Connect people, businesses and local economies through technology, governed data and helpful AI.  
**Mission:** Help people discover local value and help businesses grow digitally.

## Investment thesis

Balkan.works aims to build a local-network infrastructure: businesses create useful, current supply; consumers discover and act; governed activity improves relevance and future intelligence; better outcomes reinforce participation.

`Local businesses + consumers + governed data + accountable AI → regional digital economy platform`

The thesis must be supported by evidence from an initial city/cities, including activation, retention, business value and a credible unit-economics model. It is not proven by strategy documents alone.

## Recommended data-room sections

### 1. Corporate and governance

Include verified company registration, ownership/cap-table records, board/decision records, IP assignments, founder/employee/contractor agreements, material contracts and an authorized corporate-contact list. Mark missing or pending documents clearly; do not infer legal status from product documentation.

### 2. Market and strategy

Include the investment thesis, target market definition, city-entry criteria, customer research, competitive assessment, partner pipeline, go-to-market plan and growth-loop hypotheses. Separate sourced market facts from assumptions and label the date/source for each external claim.

### 3. Product and customer evidence

Include product architecture, MVP scope, roadmap, controlled demos/screenshots, pilot results, user research, support themes, business onboarding funnel and cohort metrics. Clearly distinguish **implemented**, **in development**, **planned** and **hypothesis**. Do not expose personal customer data or confidential business information.

### 4. Technology and security

Include architecture diagrams, repository/readme overview, API and database documentation, deployment approach, testing evidence, dependency/license inventory, security policy, access-control overview, backup/recovery evidence and known technical risks. Credentials, production connection strings, private keys and security-sensitive exploit details are never included.

### 5. Data, AI and privacy

Include data map, event taxonomy, data-governance policy, privacy notices/processes, AI architecture/evaluation plan, retention/deletion model, vendor/subprocessor list and access/audit approach. State explicitly which AI services are planned versus live and what data controls have been verified.

### 6. Financial and commercial

Include budget and burn history, cash/runway, financial model assumptions, actual revenue where applicable, pipeline definitions, pricing/packaging, cohorts, CAC/LTV methodology, churn/retention, contracts and material liabilities. Forecasts must state assumptions, scenario ranges, owner and revision date; they are not factual performance.

### 7. Legal and compliance

Include terms, privacy/cookie materials, business terms, data-processing agreements, trademark/IP status, material regulatory analysis, dispute/litigation status and compliance gaps with owner/action plan. Obtain jurisdiction-specific legal review before relying on any framework as compliance proof.

## Controlled folder structure

When the data room is actually opened for diligence, organize it with a permissioned, time-bounded folder structure:

```text
BALKAN.WORKS_INVESTOR_ROOM/
├── 01_COMPANY/             # Registration, ownership, cap table, corporate history
├── 02_VISION_STRATEGY/     # Vision, Master Plan, market and competitive strategy
├── 03_PRODUCT/             # MVP, roadmap, demo evidence, flows and product learning
├── 04_TECHNOLOGY/          # Architecture, code/IP evidence, security and technical decisions
├── 05_MARKET/              # Market research, customer evidence, competition and trends
├── 06_GROWTH/              # GTM, sales, partnerships, acquisition and cohorts
├── 07_FINANCE/             # P&L, cash flow, budget, forecast, revenue and runway
├── 08_LEGAL/               # Contracts, privacy, IP, compliance and material matters
├── 09_TEAM/                # Founders, key hires, organization and hiring plan
└── 10_DUE_DILIGENCE/       # Q&A log, checklists, risk register and evidence tracker
```

Each folder has an owner, sensitivity classification, current date, external-share status and review date. Corporate records, cap table, contracts, financial statements, legal opinions and personal information are shared only through approved access controls and qualified legal/finance guidance.

## Investor updates and relationship system

Maintain a permissioned relationship register with contact, organization, stage/interest, prior discussion, materials shared, confidentiality status, next step, owner and date. Do not store unnecessary personal data or sensitive investor notes without an approved purpose/retention rule.

For appropriate existing investors/advisers, a monthly update includes:

- **Progress:** product, business, team or operating milestones completed.
- **Metrics:** dated/cohort-defined user, business, retention and financial evidence.
- **Learnings:** what the company learned and how it changed a decision.
- **Challenges:** material risks, blockers and specific help requested.
- **Next steps:** priorities and evidence expected before the next update.

Updates distinguish facts, targets and assumptions. They are not a substitute for required corporate disclosures or legal communications.

## Metrics pack

Report a consistent dated metric definition and source for:

- Consumer: registrations, activated users, WAU/MAU where applicable, search success, retention and referral.
- Business: leads, signups, activated profiles, verified businesses, current offers, retained supply and paid conversion.
- Platform: supply density, search quality, customer actions, moderation/support health and city-level network health.
- Financial: MRR/ARR if active, gross margin, CAC, LTV methodology, churn, burn and runway.

Avoid vanity metrics. Cohorts, denominators, geography, time range, exclusions and known limitations accompany every chart.

## Funding use and stage logic

Possible funding use is allocated to product reliability, mobile/web experience, measured city activation, sales/partnerships, security, support and disciplined infrastructure growth. Typical stages are pre-seed (MVP), seed (market-fit evidence), Series A (repeatable regional expansion) and later growth (ecosystem scaling). Actual raise timing, amount, valuation and security terms are board/legal/finance decisions, not determined by this document.

## Sharing and access protocol

Use a permissioned data-room provider or equivalent controlled folder. Assign an owner to each folder, classify documents by sensitivity, grant time-bounded least-privilege access, maintain a viewer/download log where appropriate, watermark sensitive exports and revoke access after the process. Share only the necessary level of detail at each diligence stage; use NDA/legal guidance where appropriate. Never upload secrets, live credentials, unrestricted production exports or personal data without a documented lawful basis and security review.

## Due-diligence readiness checklist

- Corporate ownership and IP evidence verified.
- Product/roadmap documents dated and internally consistent.
- Architecture, deployment, test and security evidence separated from future plans.
- Metrics reproducible from documented sources.
- Financial assumptions and actuals clearly separated.
- Material contracts, risks and gaps disclosed accurately.
- Privacy, legal and regulatory materials reviewed by qualified advisers.
- Access model, document owner and update date assigned for every folder.

## Final statement

Balkan.works is presented as a scalable platform opportunity through disciplined evidence: a local network, modular technology, responsible data/AI, clear commercial model and transparent risk management. The data room makes that evidence reviewable without compromising trust or security.
