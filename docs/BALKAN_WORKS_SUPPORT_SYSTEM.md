# Balkan.works Support System

## Purpose

Define a scalable support model for consumers, businesses and approved partners. Support is part of the product: it resolves issues, teaches people how to succeed and turns recurring problems into product improvements.

## Objectives

Provide clear, timely and secure help; protect accounts and business data; capture useful feedback; and route incidents to the right owner without exposing private information or promising unsupported outcomes.

## Supported audiences

| Audience | Typical needs | Primary outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Consumers | Account access, discovery, offers, reports and notifications | Resolve safely and restore confidence |
| Businesses | Profile, verification, offers, analytics and plan questions | Activate and operate a successful local presence |
| Partners | Approved integrations, collaboration and technical questions | Clear owner, documented scope and secure resolution |

## Channels

- **In-app support:** contextual messages, issue reporting and help entry points.
- **Email:** detailed or business-related requests, with verified sender handling.
- **Help center:** searchable guides, FAQs, tutorials and status information.
- **Community support (future):** trained local ambassadors under clear escalation and privacy rules.

Channels should converge on one ticket record. Do not ask users to share passwords, tokens, payment details or unnecessary personal data in a support conversation.

## Service model

`Requester → intake and categorization → support owner → specialist → engineering/operations → resolution → feedback and knowledge update`

| Level | Responsibility | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| L1 — General support | Common questions, navigation, basic account guidance | Login help, finding an offer, reporting content |
| L2 — Business success | Business profile, offers, verification process and permitted analytics | Profile completion, offer publishing, verification status |
| L3 — Technical/operations | Product defects, integration incidents and systemic issues | API failure, data issue, platform outage |

Access is least-privilege. Support staff use role-limited tools; high-risk account, verification and moderation actions require documented authorization and audit logging.

## Ticket lifecycle

Each request receives an ID, reporter/contact reference, category, priority, status, assignee, timestamps, a privacy-safe history and resolution code.

`New → Assigned → In progress → Waiting for requester/dependency → Resolved → Closed`

Reopening is possible when a reported problem persists. The requester receives meaningful status updates without revealing internal security or personal data.

## Priority and escalation

| Priority | Definition | Handling expectation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| P1 — Critical | Platform outage, suspected security incident or broad severe impact | Immediate incident escalation, regular status communication and post-incident review |
| P2 — High | Important function unavailable for a meaningful group or business-critical blocked flow | Prompt triage, named owner and progress tracking |
| P3 — Normal | Standard individual product/support issue | Timely response through the normal queue |
| P4 — Low | Question, suggestion or minor improvement | Acknowledge, route and prioritize with product feedback |

Actual response and resolution targets are operational commitments to define by market, staffing and service hours. Avoid publishing unattainable SLAs. Security reports bypass normal queues and follow the incident-response process.

## Knowledge base and self-service

Maintain versioned, searchable articles for consumer use, business onboarding, verification, offers, account safety, technical troubleshooting and known service status. Each article has an owner, review date, locale/language and a clear next step. Support agents turn recurring tickets into improved documentation or product work.

## Automation and future AI

Safe automation can acknowledge receipt, classify the request, suggest approved knowledge-base material and send status changes. A future AI assistant may draft responses or guide a user, but it must identify itself, use approved sources, protect context, provide escalation to a human and never perform account, moderation, financial or publishing actions without authorization and confirmation.

## Feedback loop

`Support signal → categorized evidence → product/engineering/operations owner → fix or decision → requester update → knowledge and metric update`

Support dashboards track volume, priority mix, first-response time, time to resolution, reopen rate, satisfaction, top issue categories, incident trends and business activation blockers. Metrics are aggregated and privacy-aware; they inform product priorities rather than becoming a reason to close tickets prematurely.

## Business success support

Businesses receive onboarding help, explainable profile-quality guidance, offer assistance and factual performance interpretation. Support explains available plans and features accurately; it does not pressure businesses into premium products or claim results that cannot be evidenced.

## Team evolution

| Stage | Model |
| --- | --- |
| MVP | Founder or small generalist team, documented escalation paths and a focused knowledge base |
| Growth | Dedicated L1/L2 ownership, technical escalation rotation and customer-success coverage |
| Regional scale | Local-language support, specialized operations and regional quality/incident management |

## Final vision

Balkan.works support becomes a reason people trust and stay with the platform: human, knowledgeable, secure and directly connected to continuous product improvement.
