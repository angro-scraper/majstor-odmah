# Balkan.works Launch Plan

## Principle

Do not launch technology for its own sake. Launch a useful local network: a user must find real value immediately and a business must see a clear path to a result.

## Phased path

`Build → Test → Pilot city → Measure → Improve → Scale`

## Phase 0 — Foundation (0–30 days)

Complete the repository/runtime baseline, database migration process, authentication, shared UI system, deployment environments, monitoring plan and backup plan. In parallel, select one launch city, initial categories and a target list of 200 prospective businesses. Prepare partner material and a repeatable onboarding process.

**Exit condition:** a secure, reviewable development/staging baseline exists; no claim of production readiness without a verified deployment and migration path.

## Phase 1 — MVP build (30–90 days)

Deliver the smallest complete loop:

- consumer: registration, profile, location, search, businesses and offers;
- business: profile, offer creation and outcome dashboard;
- admin: business approval and content moderation.

**Exit condition:** testable end-to-end paths, reviewable security controls, analytics events, support workflow and real content onboarding.

## Phase 2 — Closed pilot (90–120 days)

Run one city with approximately 500 invited users and 50 active businesses. Users test discovery and feedback loops; businesses publish current offers and review outcomes. Track registrations, activation, retention, offer freshness, searches with useful results, views/actions and support burden.

**Exit condition:** repeat use, active supply, reliable support and evidence that businesses receive value.

## Phase 3 — Public launch (120–180 days)

Open the pilot city only after the product is stable, local supply is meaningful, support is staffed, monitoring/backups are verified and analytics are operating. Use partner locations, QR material, local content, community creators, referrals and launch events. Marketing should always reflect live, local availability.

First revenue can come from transparent premium profiles, featured offers and digital marketing packages. Do not introduce transactions or payment claims before the relevant provider, legal and consumer-protection controls are ready.

## Phase 4 — Scale (6–12 months)

After the city model is repeatable, expand city by city. Add Save Food, consent-aware recommendations, rewards and advanced analytics only when they improve a demonstrated loop. Target outcomes are 10 cities, 1,000+ active businesses and 100,000 users — not broad but inactive coverage.

## Launch checklist

### Product

- Stable app/API, versioned migrations and tested recovery path.
- Auth, moderation and permissions reviewed.
- Analytics, error reporting and uptime monitoring active.

### Supply and operations

- Active, verified businesses with fresh offers.
- User/business support, escalation and incident process.
- Local partner and onboarding ownership.

### Go/no-go metrics

- Useful search/offer results, activation and retention.
- Business offer activity and measured outcomes.
- Acceptable support, incident and operational load.

## Failure modes to avoid

Do not ship a wide feature list before a useful local loop, expand before the first city works, ignore business supply, or make onboarding complex. Reassess scope using evidence, then improve before scaling.
