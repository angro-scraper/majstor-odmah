# Balkan.works Financial Model

## Purpose

Define the financial operating model for Balkan.works: how the company plans investment, costs, revenue, growth and a path toward sustainable profitability. This is an internal planning framework, not investment advice, a fundraising offer or a forecast of guaranteed results.

## Principle

First build a useful local network; then scale revenue that follows demonstrated value. Financial decisions must be tied to evidence from customers, unit economics and local market health — not downloads, vanity metrics or unvalidated assumptions.

`Foundation → MVP → market-fit validation → growth → scale`

## Cost model by phase

| Phase | Objective | Primary cost groups | Financial question |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Foundation | Build a secure usable core | Product/design/engineering, cloud, tooling, legal/operations | Can the team deliver the MVP within controlled burn? |
| MVP launch | Activate first city supply and users | Product quality, testing, local sales/onboarding, support and focused marketing | Are businesses and users reaching first value? |
| Market fit | Prove repeatable demand and willingness to pay | Sales, customer success, measurement, reliability and retention | Does activated supply retain and create measurable business value? |
| Growth | Repeat a proven city playbook | City operations, partnerships, product capacity and acquisition | Does the model work at acceptable CAC and retention? |
| Scale | Expand only proven loops | Multi-market operations, resilient infrastructure, compliance and leadership | Can growth preserve margin, quality and trust? |

## Cost structure

- **Technology:** engineering, product/design, cloud/database/storage, monitoring, security, testing and maintenance.
- **People:** product, engineering, sales, customer success/support, operations and necessary legal/finance expertise.
- **Growth:** local research, sales activity, partner programs, content and measurable marketing.
- **Operations/compliance:** administration, accounting, contracts, insurance where needed and regulatory readiness.

Costs are reviewed by city, product module, fixed versus variable nature and their direct link to an operating outcome. Do not treat future payments, AI or marketplace costs as negligible; they need separate vendor, compliance and risk analysis before activation.

## Revenue model

| Revenue stream | Value exchanged | Release condition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Business subscriptions | Business profile, tools and eligible analytics | Activated businesses demonstrate ongoing value and plan entitlements are ready |
| Premium visibility | Optional promotion and local discoverability | Clear labeling, measurement, fairness policy and pricing |
| Local campaigns/advertising | Relevant, transparent campaign reach | User trust controls, moderation and reporting |
| Marketplace/Save Food commission | Completed eligible transaction | Payment, legal, refunds, reconciliation and support controls |
| AI services | Approval-based assistance or analytics | Data governance, quality evaluation, pricing and disclosures |
| Approved insights/enterprise products | Privacy-preserving business value | Aggregation, contracts, privacy/security review |

Free plans support activation. Paid plans are offered only when the business can see a specific incremental value; unreleased integrations or capabilities must never be sold as current functionality.

## Core operating metrics

| Metric | Meaning | Decision use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MRR / ARR | Recurring subscription revenue, monthly/annualized | Recurring revenue trend and forecast discipline |
| CAC | Fully loaded cost to acquire an activated customer | Channel and city efficiency |
| LTV | Expected gross-profit contribution over customer lifetime | Sustainable acquisition ceiling |
| Churn / retention | Customer loss and continued active use | Product-market fit and revenue durability |
| Gross margin | Revenue less direct service delivery cost | Pricing and infrastructure sustainability |
| Payback period | Time for gross profit to recover CAC | Cash efficiency |
| Burn and runway | Net cash use and months of funding remaining | Hiring, scope and financing decisions |

Track consumer and business metrics separately. A business signup is not a customer; activation, recurring use, offer freshness and retained value are required to interpret revenue potential.

## Unit economics

For a subscription line, the basic model is:

`Active paying businesses × average recurring revenue per business = MRR`

Evaluate this alongside direct support, infrastructure, payment/transaction, campaign and account-management costs. Sustainable growth seeks positive contribution margin and a healthy relationship between LTV and CAC, but inputs must be measured by cohort rather than assumed. Marketplace and payments require separate take-rate, loss, refund and reconciliation models.

## Forecast and scenarios

Maintain conservative, expected and aggressive scenarios. Each scenario declares active cities, activated businesses, paid conversion, average revenue, churn, hiring plan, marketing/sales spend, cloud/service costs, runway and dependencies. Use ranges and sensitivity analysis for uncertain inputs. Review monthly against actuals and replace assumptions with observed cohort data.

| Scenario | Focus | Guardrail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Conservative | Controlled burn and local retention | Do not expand before one city is healthy |
| Expected | Repeat validated city playbook | Maintain service quality and unit economics |
| Aggressive | Faster expansion after evidence | Do not trade compliance, trust or cash discipline for speed |

## Break-even and capital allocation

Break-even means recurring and transactional gross profit reliably covers product, infrastructure and operating costs at the defined scope. Investment is allocated to product reliability, local supply/growth, key hires, security and infrastructure according to measurable milestones. Funding decisions use a documented budget, owner, approval path and runway impact.

## Financial controls

Maintain monthly actual-versus-budget reporting, cash forecast, approved spending limits, vendor/contract review, revenue recognition policy, tax/accounting review, access separation and audit-ready records. Financial, wallet and payment data require specialized controls and qualified accounting/legal/payment-provider guidance before launch.

## Review cadence

- Weekly: cash-sensitive exceptions, major spend and material revenue risks.
- Monthly: P&L, cash/runway, MRR, cohorts, CAC, retention and budget variance.
- Quarterly: scenario refresh, pricing/packaging review, city investment decisions and hiring plan.

## Final vision

Balkan.works is built to become a company with repeatable, value-backed revenue — SaaS, visibility, transactions and intelligent tools — while maintaining disciplined costs, clear controls and sustainable local network growth.
