# Balkan.works Go-To-Market Strategy

## Principle

Win one place before expanding: one city → validated local success → more cities → country → regional network. Growth comes from a useful local network, not ungrounded advertising reach.

## City selection

Select a pilot city using population/reach, business density, digital adoption, partner potential, community activity, local operating access and cost. Score candidates consistently and choose the city with the highest probability of dense, repeatable supply and demand.

## Supply-first business acquisition

Before broad consumer campaigns, onboard 100 active businesses across food (restaurants, bakeries, cafés), retail (shops/local brands) and services (salons, repair/professional services). The business promise is simple: **Balkan.works helps local customers find you.**

`Qualified contact → profile → first offer → activation → measured outcome`

An active business has a verified, usable profile and a current offer, not merely an imported record. Direct outreach, local partners, referrals and ambassadors build supply.

## Consumer acquisition

Target the first 1,000 active users only after enough useful supply exists. Channels:

- Partner distribution: QR placement and staff-supported onboarding at active businesses.
- Referrals: transparent, non-spam incentives when rewards are available.
- Local community: events, groups, ambassadors and city content.
- Content: real local offers, business stories and useful city guidance.

The first-five-minute flow is location selection → useful offer/business discovery → save or use an offer. A download without this activation is not success.

## Launch week

Coordinate partner posts, social/community content, local media outreach, a launch event where appropriate and offline QR placements. Every promotion must be able to direct users to current, actual local inventory/offers. Capture support feedback and fix activation barriers daily.

## Retention loops

Consumers return for fresh offers, local recommendations and savings. Businesses remain for visible reach, simple publishing and measurable customer interest. Notifications are optional and relevant; offer freshness and search success are the durable retention mechanism.

## Local operating model

A city ambassador supports businesses, community feedback and local activation. As a city proves itself, add accountable business/community operations rather than a broad national team without local context.

## First 90 days

| Period | Focus | Evidence target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Days 1–30 | Select city, business outreach, onboard/activate supply | 50 active businesses |
| Days 31–60 | Controlled consumer activation and behavior measurement | 500 activated users |
| Days 61–90 | Improve loop, test paid value and partner motion | 100 active businesses + 1,000 active users |

Targets are planning hypotheses, not public performance claims. Adjust from measured onboarding, retention and business outcome data.

## Expansion gate

Do not enter a second city until the first has active businesses, active/repeating users, current local content, reliable support, understood unit economics and documented operating playbook.
