# Balkan.works — Investor Narrative Draft

> Internal product narrative. This is not an offer to sell securities, financial advice, a valuation, market-sizing evidence or a revenue forecast. Any external investor material must use verified data, financial assumptions and legal review.

## Mission

Connect people, local businesses and the regional economy through one intelligent digital platform.

## The problem

Consumers use separate tools for local discovery, offers, food surplus, services and business communication. Small and medium businesses use disconnected marketing, sales, inventory and analytics tools. The result is fragmented demand, fragmented supply and limited useful local insight.

## The solution

Balkan.works is a shared Core — Balkan ID, businesses, location, discovery, offers, trust, notifications and governed data — with specialized modules for Save Food, Digital Flyers, Opsnestone, Marketplace, Wallet and intelligence. Users receive useful local discovery; businesses receive digital distribution and operational tools; partners receive governed integration paths.

## Who it serves

- **Consumers:** local offers, savings, trusted local businesses and simpler discovery.
- **Small and medium businesses:** profiles, customer reach, offers, campaigns and business tools.
- **Strategic partners:** a locally relevant distribution and integration network.

## Advantage to prove

The core hypothesis is a local network effect:

`more active businesses → more fresh offers → more useful consumer visits → stronger business outcomes → more businesses`

The platform's advantage is not a feature list. It must prove dense local supply, repeat consumer value, trusted data practices, low-friction business onboarding and measured outcomes before expanding. AI strengthens discovery and operations only when it is grounded, permissioned and measurably helpful.

## Revenue model

Potential revenue layers are business subscriptions, transparent premium visibility, transaction fees where compliant payments exist, premium AI/business automation and enterprise partnerships. The free layer must remain useful; personal data is not sold. See the monetization model for guardrails and phased activation.

## Go-to-market

Start with one city, acquire an active local supply base, run a closed pilot, measure repeat value, then replicate a documented city playbook. The initial loop is businesses and offers first; consumer acquisition follows only when supply is genuinely useful.

## Product sequence

1. Core: identity, businesses, offers, location, search, moderation and analytics.
2. Growth: Save Food, recommendations and business tools.
3. Expansion: Marketplace, Wallet and approved partner integrations.
4. Intelligence: governed agents and market intelligence.

## Capital use framework

Any future funding plan should allocate against validated milestones: product and infrastructure delivery, one-city supply acquisition, product/engineering/security capacity, customer success and evidence-based growth. Capital planning must include runway, hiring plan, unit economics and downside scenarios.

## Metrics to validate

Track active users, activation, retention, active businesses, offer freshness, search success, business conversion, MRR/ARPU where applicable, contribution margin, churn and support quality. Do not replace these with vanity downloads or unverified total-addressable-market claims.

## Why now — thesis to validate

Digital adoption, local business demand for accessible tools, consumer search for value and advances in AI create an opportunity for an integrated local platform. The investment case depends on proof that Balkan.works can create a trusted, repeatable city network.

## Long-term vision

Become digital infrastructure for everyday local life and business in the region: one identity, one network and many permissioned, useful services.
