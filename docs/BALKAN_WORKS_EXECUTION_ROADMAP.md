# Balkan.works Execution Roadmap

## Purpose

Turn the Balkan.works vision into a phased execution roadmap: build the MVP, validate the market, activate the first users and businesses, and create the evidence required for sustainable growth.

## Principle

Prove value before expanding capability. Optimize for successful local connections between people and businesses, not the number of screens, modules, downloads or countries announced.

**North Star:** increase the number and quality of successful, trusted connections between users and relevant local businesses.

## Phase 0 — Preparation (2–4 weeks)

**Goal:** define product, pilot market, technical foundation and priorities.

**Deliverables:** completed/organized core documentation, validated MVP scope, architecture and environment plan, prioritized/backlog-ready work, initial design patterns, pilot-city criteria and first business acquisition plan.

**Exit gate:** one accountable MVP definition and delivery plan exist; risks/dependencies are visible; the team can demonstrate the core technical foundation internally.

## Phase 1 — MVP build (8–12 weeks)

**Goal:** a consumer can find a business; a business can create a profile; an authorized administrator can manage quality and access.

| Consumer | Business | Admin |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Registration/login, profile, location, categories, search, business detail and favorites | Business signup/profile, contact, location, opening hours, images and offer readiness | User/business review, approval/moderation, permissions and audit trail |

The MVP is successful when core flows work reliably with valid data, permissions, error states, monitoring and support ownership. Current code/documentation foundation is not treated as completed production functionality until its dependencies, migrations, tests and deployment are executed and verified.

## Phase 2 — Private beta (4–8 weeks)

**Goal:** test real behavior in one pilot city before public expansion.

Directional targets are around 100 businesses and 500 invited/activated users, adjusted for city capacity and content quality. Work includes direct business onboarding, local outreach, supported user testing, product instrumentation and weekly feedback cycles.

**Evidence sought:** useful search results, active/current profiles, first business value moments, user feedback, support/moderation signals and early return behavior.

## Phase 3 — Public launch (2–3 months)

**Goal:** open the proven local experience to the public with enough accurate supply and operational readiness.

Priorities are local SEO/content, partnerships, transparent marketing, support, moderation and product reliability. Directional market goals such as 1,000 businesses and 10,000 users are hypotheses, not launch criteria; activation, retention, quality and local density determine readiness.

## Phase 4 — Growth (6–12 months)

**Goal:** validate a repeatable business model in a healthy local network.

Introduce only evidence-backed paid value: premium business profiles, clear promotions and useful analytics. Measure paid conversion, retention, gross margin, CAC/LTV methodology and business outcomes. Expand city/category coverage only when the original loop remains healthy.

## Phase 5 — Scale (12–24 months)

**Goal:** repeat the city playbook across locations/countries without losing trust or operational control.

Add localized language/taxonomy/policy support, local partners, country operations, mobile release maturity, advanced analytics and grounded AI recommendations when their readiness gates are met. New locations require supply, support, compliance and economics plans — not merely demand forecasts.

## Phase 6 — Transaction layer

**Goal:** let users complete eligible needs inside the platform.

Potential capabilities include reservations, purchases and payments. They require transaction lifecycle, merchant/consumer policies, payment provider, refunds/disputes, reconciliation, fraud/security, legal and support controls. Revenue may include transparent commissions, but no transactional function is launched before these prerequisites exist.

## Phase 7 — AI platform

**Goal:** make Balkan.works a governed intelligence layer.

Sequence: AI search → recommendations → grounded assistant → business assistance → permissioned agents. Each release requires data purpose/consent, gateway controls, quality evaluation, safety tests, monitoring, human confirmation for consequential actions and rollback capability.

## Time horizons

| Horizon | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| First 30 days | Scope, backlog, architecture, design/system setup and pilot preparation |
| First 90 days | MVP vertical flows, internal quality checks and first business supply preparation |
| First 12 months | Pilot/public learning, city health, retention and first validated paid value |
| Beyond | Repeatable expansion, selected transactions and governed intelligence |

## What not to build early

Do not start with a broad marketplace, complex autonomous agents, enterprise-heavy customization, wallet/financial functionality or international expansion. Those capabilities require trust, operational depth, regulatory work, data quality and a proven local core.

## Roadmap governance

Each phase has an owner, scope, evidence target, risks, budget/capacity view and formal go/iterate/stop decision. The Master Roadmap, Execution Plan, First 180 Days Plan and Module Roadmap remain linked planning documents: this roadmap is the concise sequence, while they provide the detailed operating gates.

## Final rule

Never optimize for product size. Optimize for the real, measurable value users and businesses receive from a trusted local network.
