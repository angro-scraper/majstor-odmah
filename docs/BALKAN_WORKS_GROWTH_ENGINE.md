# Balkan.works Growth Engine

## Purpose

Define the operating system that turns each new user, business and partner into more value for the Balkan.works local network. This complements the Growth Loops document with practical city, channel and community execution.

## Growth vision

Do not merely buy users through advertising. Build a network: more active users attract more quality local businesses and current offers; more useful supply creates more value and sustainable demand.

`Useful local users → active businesses → current offers/content → successful connections → stronger network`

## Six growth pillars

| Pillar | Purpose | Quality guardrail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Local network | Build dense, useful supply in one city | Do not expand a city before the first local loop works |
| Referral | Let people/businesses share genuine value | Transparent incentives, fraud controls and retained activation |
| Business acquisition | Create active profiles and current offers | Measure first value/retention, not signups alone |
| Content | Become a useful local discovery resource | Accurate, fresh, non-duplicated and source-aware content |
| Partnerships | Access trusted local communities | Shared goals, attribution, privacy and clear owner |
| Community | Build trust and feedback through local people | Training, moderation, safety and accountable escalation |

## City-by-city strategy

Start with one city. Before public demand scaling, prepare a focused, quality supply across initial categories such as food, local retail and local services. A directional launch mix may include 100–200 businesses, a meaningful set of current offers and around 1,000 activated users; city capacity, supply quality, retention and operations determine readiness rather than an arbitrary formula.

### City launch sequence

1. Map local categories, partners, demand and operational constraints.
2. Acquire/activate accurate business profiles and current offers.
3. Prepare content, support, moderation, analytics and local partner communication.
4. Run a controlled launch with users/partners and collect feedback.
5. Improve activation/retention before increasing spend or geography.

## Referral system

Consumers can share a useful business, offer, guide or invitation. Businesses can introduce another relevant business. Future rewards may include transparent points, benefits, eligible premium time or promotion, but only after reward economics, terms, anti-abuse controls and feature flags are ready.

Measure shares, accepted invitations, activated referrals, retained referred cohorts, incentive cost and fraud/abuse. Referral volume without activation is not a growth win.

## Business acquisition engine

`Qualified contact → discovery/demo → profile setup → quality/verification review → first offer/value → retained active business`

Focus early on business categories with frequent local need and ability to keep information current. The message is not “be on an app”; it is “help relevant local customers find you and understand the interest you receive.” Use the Sales Playbook and First 100 Businesses Plan for field process, onboarding and quality standards.

## Content and local discovery

Balkan.works can become a useful local media/discovery layer through current offers, business stories, city/category guides, events when activated and practical business digitalization content. Local SEO uses well-maintained city/category pages and clear editorial methodology. Do not fabricate rankings, publish thin pages or use stale business data to chase traffic.

## Partnership engine

Potential partner classes include local business associations, cities/community organizations, retail/food networks, universities, local media, banks/telecoms and technology providers. Early partners are selected for trusted local distribution and shared measurable outcomes; larger integrations wait for technical, legal, privacy and commercial readiness.

Each partnership has an owner, target audience, value exchange, data boundary, launch plan, attribution method, success metric and exit/review condition.

## Community engine

Local ambassadors — potentially students, local creators and active community members — can explain the product, surface feedback, help businesses understand onboarding and share useful local stories. They do not moderate sensitive cases, make verification decisions or access private data without specific role/training/permission.

Future gamification may recognize useful exploration or community contribution. It must not encourage spam, fake reviews, unsafe behavior or misleading business rankings.

## Growth loops in action

| Loop | Current/future mechanism | Release gate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Offers | Current business offers create consumer discovery and business outcome | Fresh supply, analytics and support capacity |
| Save Food | Future surplus/reservation creates savings and waste reduction | Food operations, reservation/payment/legal readiness |
| Business software | Future Opsnestone data improves business workflows/relevance | Tenant isolation, consent, business value and integrations |

## Marketing channels

Use a measured mix of social/community distribution, SEO/content, local partnerships, QR/offline material at partner locations and, after validation, carefully attributed paid campaigns. Every channel is evaluated by activated/retained users and active business supply, not impressions/clicks alone.

## Metrics

- **Users:** activated registrations, successful connections, retention, referral quality and support satisfaction.
- **Businesses:** qualified leads, active/verified-eligible profiles, current offers, first value, retention and customer interest.
- **Growth economics:** attributable CAC, LTV methodology, payback, channel contribution and viral coefficient with clear formula.
- **Network health:** city/category supply density, freshness, search success, trust/moderation health and local partner contribution.

## First 100 days

| Period | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| Days 1–30 | Core product evidence, initial local business supply and measurement readiness |
| Days 31–60 | Controlled pilot-city activation, current offers/content and direct feedback |
| Days 61–100 | Improve validated loops; test eligible referral/partner/content tactics before broad marketing |

## Final vision

Balkan.works grows city by city as a network, not a campaign. Every quality business, useful local action and trusted partner becomes a node that makes the next connection more valuable.
