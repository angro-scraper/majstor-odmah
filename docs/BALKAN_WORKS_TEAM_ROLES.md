# Balkan.works Team Roles

## Principle

Hire for the next validated constraint, not for titles. Each role must increase product speed, quality, local network strength or sustainable revenue.

## Stage 1 — Founder team (2–4 people)

| Role | Accountable for |
| --- | --- |
| Founder / CEO | Strategy, partnerships, investment process, decisive priorities and culture |
| CTO / Tech Lead | Architecture, security, engineering process and technical decisions |
| Product Lead | Roadmap, user experience, prioritization and discovery/testing of ideas |
| Growth / Business Lead | Business acquisition, users, partnerships and early sales motion |

Responsibilities can be combined, but ownership of an outcome remains explicit.

## Stage 2 — MVP team (5–10 people)

Engineering adds backend (API/data/business rules), frontend (web/UI/components), mobile (iOS/Android experience), UI/UX design and QA/test automation. Business adds sales, community and marketing roles to acquire active supply, support ambassadors and communicate useful local value.

## Stage 3 — Growth team (10–30 people)

Add a data analyst for metrics/behavior/business insight, customer support for user/business resolution, partnership management for strategic relationships and content/SEO/social capacity for local reach. City operations grows only where the local model is working.

## Stage 4 — Scale team

Add leadership only when there are teams/outcomes to lead: VP Engineering, Head of AI, Head of Sales, Head of Marketing and Operations Director. Add Legal Counsel for contracts/regulation/privacy and Finance leadership for budgets, accounting and investment operations as the risk and transaction volume justify it.

## First-ten-hire reference sequence

1. CTO / technical leadership
2. Backend developer
3. Frontend or mobile developer, based on the active product channel
4. Product designer
5. Business development
6. Sales representative
7. Marketing specialist
8. QA engineer
9. Customer support
10. Data/AI engineer when data/product prerequisites exist

This is a guide, not a fixed order: a company with an acute supply problem may hire business/sales capacity before another engineering specialization.

## Working culture

Ownership, rapid learning, quality, transparency and user/business value are the standard. Every role has measurable outcome metrics and documented handoffs. Avoid oversized management, broad specialist hiring without product evidence or growth that outruns support and trust.
