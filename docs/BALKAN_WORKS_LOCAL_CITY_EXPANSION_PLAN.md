# Balkan.works Local City Expansion Plan

## Principle

Grow a network, not territory: validate one strong local marketplace, reproduce its operating playbook in the next city, then build a country network from working city nodes.

## City launch sequence

`Research → Business acquisition → Community building → User launch → Optimization → Expansion`

## City selection scorecard

Score each candidate city against local business density, food/retail/service supply, digital readiness, student/community network, partner availability, operating cost, regulatory readiness and local team access. Select a single launch city with the highest chance of a dense, repeat-use network.

## Supply before demand

Before consumer marketing, target at least 100 activated businesses, 500 live offers and a reliable mix of food, local retail, services and events. “Activated” means a verified profile plus at least one active offer or package, not merely a registration.

## Local acquisition playbook

`Qualified contact → Demonstration → Registration → Profile completion → First offer → First measured outcome`

The launch team prioritizes restaurants, cafés, bakeries, markets, local shops, salons, repair/services and local culture/sports organizers. City ambassadors help source local content and partner feedback but do not receive unrestricted platform or user data.

## Consumer launch

After supply quality is proven, use partner QR placements, local content, referrals, creator collaborations and an in-city launch campaign. Marketing promises must match live availability; no generic “super app” campaign substitutes for useful local offers.

## City operations dashboard

| Area | Measure |
| --- | --- |
| Users | Registrations, activation, weekly/monthly actives, repeat use |
| Businesses | Verified and active businesses, offer freshness, campaign outcomes |
| Network | Supply density, searches with useful results, reservations/interactions |
| Economics | Revenue, acquisition cost, partner retention and operating cost |

The city score combines active users, active businesses, engagement, retained supply and sustainable revenue. It is an operational decision tool, not a public ranking.

## Expansion gate

Do not launch the next city until the current one has regular active users, fresh local content, an operating acquisition process, reliable support/moderation and sustainable unit economics. Document the playbook and failure modes before replication.

## Localization and regional rollout

Each country configuration supports language, currency, local categories, tax/legal requirements and locally relevant partners. The intended sequence is Serbia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Slovenia and then diaspora markets, subject to validated city readiness rather than calendar pressure.

## First-year target

One validated city with 100–300 active businesses and 10,000 users is a stronger foundation than thin coverage across many cities. The growth flywheel is: more relevant businesses → more current offers → more useful consumer visits → stronger business outcomes → more businesses.
