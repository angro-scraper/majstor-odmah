# Investor and Launch Readiness

## Evidence before narrative

Investor, partner and launch material must separate three states:

- **Live metric:** measured from a documented source and time range.
- **Target:** a future gate or operating objective.
- **Pipeline:** a prospective user, business or partner; never counted as active revenue or an active integration.

This prevents the launch story from overstating traction and keeps future due diligence straightforward.

## Data room minimum

| Area | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Product | current public routes, API contract, feature flag state and tested end-to-end paths |
| Market launch | pilot-city scorecard, supply segmentation, launch calendar and named operational ownership |
| Growth | channel attribution, activation cohort, retention cohorts, referrals and successful connections |
| Business network | active/verified businesses, offer freshness, business first-value and support outcomes |
| Finance | recognized subscription/transaction revenue only, reconciled costs, commission policy and provider status |
| Security | access controls, audit evidence, incident/backup procedures and privacy controls |

## Core investor dashboard

Report each metric with its city, date range, definition, denominator and known limitation:

1. active and verified businesses, current offers and supply density;
2. registered users, activated users, DAU/WAU/MAU and cohort retention;
3. successful connections, search success, qualified contacts and Save Food pickups where active;
4. referral completion and channel-to-activation efficiency;
5. recognized revenue, recurring revenue, GMV and transaction margin only after their finance controls are live;
6. support, moderation and incident trends.

## Pitch outline

1. Local discovery/supply problem and the pilot-city insight.
2. Balkan.works product loop: businesses publish value, people discover it, measured action returns value to businesses.
3. Market entry evidence: scorecard result, local supply quality and retention—not feature count.
4. Business model: transparent subscriptions, promotion and transaction fees as each regulated capability becomes ready.
5. Defensibility: trusted local network, verified data, operational playbook and consent-aware intelligence.
6. Expansion: repeat the proven city playbook; do not extrapolate untested regions as realized traction.
7. Use of funds, operating milestones and measurable risk controls.

## Pre-share review

Before sharing material externally, product, finance and legal owners confirm that any figures reconcile with source data; testimonials and partner names are authorized; the current feature flags are accurate; and all forward-looking statements are marked as targets rather than results.
