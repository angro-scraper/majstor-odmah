# Balkan.works Founder Playbook

## Purpose

Define how the founder leads Balkan.works from an idea into a sustainable company. The focus is not on any one function; it is on disciplined decisions, customer learning, local network health and accountable execution.

## Founder mission

The founder's primary responsibility is to create a system that solves a meaningful user/business problem and can grow responsibly. Programming, design, sales and marketing are important activities, but the founder owns direction, priorities, resources, culture and the decision to stop work that is not creating value.

## First rule

Do not build what people do not want. Validate the problem, validate a solution through actual use, then scale what retains. Features, hires, partnerships and fundraising follow evidence — not excitement, competition or technical novelty.

`Problem → users → businesses → retention → revenue → scale`

## First 12-month priorities

1. Understand a specific local problem and pilot city.
2. Build a focused core that makes local discovery/business participation useful.
3. Personally learn from initial businesses and users.
4. Improve activation and retention before widening the product or geography.
5. Test paid value only after outcomes are visible.
6. Establish the repeatable team, operating model and capital plan needed to scale.

## Decision framework

Before a significant feature, hire, city launch, partnership or spend decision, ask:

1. What concrete user or business problem does this solve?
2. What evidence says the target audience needs it now?
3. How does it strengthen the local network or current product loop?
4. What metric and time window will show success, harm or failure?
5. What is the smallest safe/reversible experiment?
6. Are security, privacy, support, legal and operational owners ready?

If the answer is weak for most questions, defer, reduce scope or stop. A deliberate “not now” is a core founder decision.

## Product rules

- One meaningful capability should solve one clearly defined problem.
- Build because customers need it, not solely because a competitor or technology exists.
- Prefer a usable end-to-end flow over a large set of disconnected screens.
- Protect the core: Balkan ID, local businesses, current offers, discovery, trust and support.
- Treat feedback, support tickets and real usage as product inputs; do not confuse opinions with evidence.

## Early growth and business rules

The first 100 businesses, 1,000 users and first revenue are learned through direct work: conversations, onboarding, demos, local content, partner activity and follow-up. Automate only after the motion is understood. Track active/current businesses and successful consumer discovery, not merely registrations or impressions.

Founder-led sales and support are research, not a temporary embarrassment. They reveal onboarding friction, price sensitivity, missing trust signals and which local categories can seed the network.

## Operating system

### Daily

Review user/business feedback, new/active businesses, core product health, support/moderation issues and delivery blockers. Make one decision that improves the current local loop.

### Weekly

| Review | Questions |
| --- | --- |
| Product | What did users try, complete, abandon or request? |
| Growth | Which sources delivered activated/retained users rather than traffic? |
| Business | Which businesses activated, saw first value, retained or churned? |
| Metrics | What improved, stalled or degraded? Are definitions trustworthy? |
| Delivery/risk | What is blocked? Which security, support or operational risk needs an owner? |

### Monthly

Review assumptions, cohorts, city health, budget/runway, hiring/capacity and the roadmap. Decide what to continue, fix, stop, postpone or accelerate. Record the decision and its evidence so the company learns over time.

## KPI priorities

| Stage | Leading focus |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Active, accurate local businesses and current offers |
| 2 | Activated users who find a useful result |
| 3 | Consumer/business retention and local network health |
| 4 | Value-led paid conversion, margin and revenue quality |
| 5 | Repeatable city/country expansion |

No single KPI tells the whole truth. Use cohorts, city/category context, qualitative feedback and guardrails for trust, quality and support.

## Founder time allocation

An initial directional allocation can be 40% growth/customer learning, 30% product, 20% business partnerships/sales and 10% operations. Adjust using current bottlenecks: a founder should spend time where evidence says the company is constrained, not follow a rigid formula.

## Hiring and culture

Early hires increase execution quality, customer learning or revenue capability. Prioritize product/engineering, growth and sales/customer success according to the immediate bottleneck. Avoid management layers, complex hierarchy and title-driven hiring before a real need exists.

Build a culture of ownership, transparent communication, speed with quality, continuous learning and customer respect. Security/privacy, accurate claims and healthy disagreement are part of that culture.

## Fundraising discipline

Capital is a tool, not the company goal. Raise when there is evidence of a problem, usage, retention/growth or a specific investment opportunity that capital can responsibly accelerate. Do not raise merely to fund unvalidated features, perform scale or founder ego. Use the Investor Data Room and Financial Model to separate evidence, assumptions, risks and funding use.

## Crisis/reset playbook

When growth or retention stalls: talk directly to users and businesses, inspect cohorts and support data, identify the failed assumption, remove low-value work, stabilize the core experience and test the smallest recovery experiment. Communicate clearly with the team; hiding weak signals delays recovery.

## Long-term view

Balkan.works is built over years, not by looking like a large company early. The durable asset is a trusted regional network of people, businesses, local knowledge and responsible technology.

## Final principle

Focus on users, businesses, value and execution. Build the company that deserves to become large; the rest follows from repeated correct decisions.
