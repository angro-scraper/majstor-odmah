# Balkan.works Module Roadmap

## Purpose

Define the phased development of Balkan.works modules so every addition reuses the existing network of identities, businesses, data and trust rather than becoming an isolated product.

## Core principle

Build the foundation first, then engagement and business value, then transactions and intelligence. A module moves forward only when its dependencies, security/operations requirements and local network evidence are ready.

`Core platform → business tools → transaction layer → intelligence platform`

This document guides module sequencing. The Master Roadmap and MVP/PRD remain the source for immediate release scope; any priority change is documented with evidence and ownership.

## Phase 0 — Foundation

**Required before all modules.** Balkan Core provides Balkan ID, roles/permissions, profiles, businesses and memberships, countries/cities/locations, categories, search/discovery, offers, events/analytics, notifications, moderation, feature flags, API contracts and security/operations controls.

Without accurate local supply, identity, permissions and discoverability, there is no ecosystem to connect.

## Phase 1 — MVP launch

### Module 1: Balkan.works Core — priority ★★★★★

Consumer scope: registration, location, local search, business/offer discovery and favorites. Business scope: profile, current offers and basic factual activity insight. Admin scope: content quality, business review and user management.

**Success evidence:** users find relevant local businesses; businesses are activated and visible; content is accurate/current; core flows are secure, supported and measurable.

## Phase 2 — Engagement

### Module 2: Offers Engine — priority ★★★★★

Extend offers with valid periods, promotion workflow, freshness controls, analytics and limited personalization. It is the daily reason to return only if offers remain current and business outcomes are visible.

### Module 3: Favorites & Personalization — priority ★★★★☆

Add followed businesses/categories, saved content, history and consent-aware relevance controls. Recommendations begin with transparent rules/experiments and can later use the governed AI layer.

**Gate:** sustained offer freshness, local search success, user retention signals and moderation/support capacity.

## Phase 3 — Business growth

### Module 4: Opsnestone / Business Operating System — priority ★★★★★

Incrementally add business workflows such as customer/CRM records, tasks, operational overview and approved automation. It connects through Balkan ID, business membership, API/events and tenant isolation; it does not directly couple to consumer-module tables.

### Module 5: Digital Flyers — priority ★★★★☆

Add campaign creation, eligible local targeting, promotion and performance measurement. It reuses profiles, offers, consent controls, analytics and moderation.

**Gate:** validated business onboarding, clear entitlement/pricing model, data-quality controls and a customer-success motion.

## Phase 4 — Transaction economy

### Module 6: Marketplace — priority ★★★★☆

Support products/services, ordering and eventual payment through its own domain contracts. It requires seller policy, trust/moderation, support, transaction lifecycle, legal review and payment-provider readiness.

### Module 7: Save Food — priority ★★★★☆

Support time-sensitive food surplus, special offers, reservation/pickup and local sustainability. It can use the shared offer/business/location/reward foundations but owns its package, reservation, pickup and operational rules.

**Gate:** transactional operations, safety/consumer policy, provider/legal readiness, business support and local supply density. Sequence between Marketplace and Save Food may be chosen per pilot evidence; neither is assumed part of the Core MVP.

## Phase 5 — Financial layer

### Module 8: Balkan Wallet & Rewards — priority ★★★☆☆

Potentially support a digital wallet integration, loyalty/rewards and eligible credits. Financial functionality requires regulated-provider, legal, security, ledger, fraud, accounting, reconciliation and user-support readiness. Architecture preparation does not authorize public financial features.

## Phase 6 — Intelligence platform

### Module 9: Stock Radar — priority ★★★☆☆

Provide governed market signals, trend analysis and business insights. It uses approved aggregate data, explicit sources and clear limitations; it does not offer unqualified investment advice.

### Module 10: AI Layer — strategic priority ★★★★★ long term

Deliver in stages: grounded search, transparent recommendations, approval-based assistants, business insight and only later permissioned agents. AI uses the gateway, knowledge/retrieval layer, evaluation, privacy and human-control standards.

## Dependency maps

```text
Balkan Core
  ├── Businesses and profiles
  ├── Locations/categories/search
  ├── Offers and events
  └── Trust, permissions and notifications
          ↓
    Engagement and business growth modules
          ↓
    Transaction/reservation modules
          ↓
    Governed analytics and AI
```

```text
Business → profile → current offers → customer interest → eligible transactions → governed data → business/AI insight
```

## Prioritization score

Each proposed module receives an evidence-based score across user value, business value, revenue potential, network effect, technical readiness, security/legal/operations readiness, support burden and dependency completeness. A high feature score cannot override a failed trust, compliance or local-supply gate.

## What not to build first

Do not begin with a wallet, complex autonomous agents, a broad marketplace or regulated financial functionality. These capabilities require meaningful user/business density, trust, data quality, operating processes and infrastructure. Build the local core loop before adding complexity.

## Five-year directional view

| Period | Primary focus |
| --- | --- |
| Year 1 | Core, profiles, offers and local discovery |
| Year 2 | Business tools, marketing and validated engagement loops |
| Year 3 | Selected marketplace/reservation transactions with strong operations |
| Year 4 | Governed intelligence and mature AI assistance |
| Year 5 | Repeatable regional ecosystem and partner integrations |

Dates are directional planning horizons, not delivery commitments. City readiness, product evidence, financing, regulation and team capacity determine the actual sequence.

## Final vision

Balkan.works grows as a platform: each module must strengthen the modules beneath it and leave people, businesses and local communities with more trusted value than before.
