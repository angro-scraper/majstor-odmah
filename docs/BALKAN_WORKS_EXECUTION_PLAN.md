# Balkan.works Execution Plan

## Purpose

Translate the Balkan.works vision into an operating plan that delivers a functional product, activates initial local businesses and users, and creates a repeatable growth system.

## Principle

Do not build everything. Build the smallest secure, measurable capability that proves real value for people and businesses, then improve it before expanding scope.

`Preparation → MVP development → pilot launch → validation → growth → scale`

## Phase 0 — Preparation (days 0–30)

**Objective:** establish product, technical, design and market foundations.

| Area | Outcome |
| --- | --- |
| Product | MVP PRD, user/business/admin flows, acceptance criteria and priority decision log |
| Technology | Repository, modular architecture, database plan, environment/secrets approach and test baseline |
| Design | Design-system tokens/components and core screen/flow decisions |
| Business | Pilot-city criteria, first-business list, onboarding and support plan |

**Deliverable:** an internally demonstrable foundation, not a claim of public production readiness.

## Phase 1 — MVP development (days 30–90)

| Sprint | Focus | Demonstrable outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Balkan ID, authentication, users/profiles and database foundation | Validated account and role-aware profile flow |
| 2 | Businesses, categories and locations | Pending business profile with ownership, taxonomy and local context |
| 3 | Offers and search | Authorized offer lifecycle plus useful public/local discovery |
| 4 | Admin, quality and polish | Moderation/approval path, relevant tests, errors/loading states and operational visibility |

**MVP scope:** consumer discovery, business profile/offers, search, favorites where ready, and controlled administration. Payments/wallet, broad marketplace, autonomous agents and unrelated modules remain out of scope until their own readiness gates are met.

## Phase 2 — Pilot launch (days 90–120)

**Objective:** run a controlled real-market test in one city.

Choose a city with active local businesses, reachable partners and a realistic path to current local content. A directional target may be 100 onboarded businesses and 1,000 invited/activated users, but local capacity and quality outweigh raw targets.

Daily work combines qualified business outreach, accurate profile/offer activation, local content, community/partner distribution and support. Soft launch proceeds only after quality, security, monitoring, support, moderation and rollback responsibilities are reviewed.

## Phase 3 — Validation (months 3–6)

**Objective:** establish whether the first local loop works.

Answer with cohorts and feedback: Do users find a useful result? Do they return? Do businesses become active and see customer interest? Which categories/city conditions create the best outcomes? Improve onboarding, search, offer quality and trust before adding major new modules.

| Dimension | Core evidence |
| --- | --- |
| User | activated users, search success, DAU/MAU where meaningful, retention, support feedback |
| Business | activated profiles, current offers, first-value rate, retained supply and business feedback |
| Product | completed journeys, reliability, top failures and moderation/quality indicators |
| Economics | attributable acquisition cost, cost to serve and early willingness-to-pay evidence |

## Phase 4 — Growth (months 6–18)

**Objective:** repeat a proven local model without weakening quality.

Add cities only after a documented city gate: active supply density, repeat consumer use, local operations/support capacity and acceptable economics. Strengthen business onboarding, analytics and Digital Flyers/Business Tools according to validated demand. AI begins with grounded search/recommendations only after data quality, consent, evaluation and gateway controls are ready.

## Phase 5 — Scale (18+ months)

**Objective:** become a multi-city, multi-country platform through repeatable operations.

Expand localized language, taxonomy, currency/tax and policy support with country review. Introduce enterprise partnerships, selected transactional modules, wallet/payment integrations and advanced agents only when the associated legal, security, provider, ledger/reconciliation, support and trust controls are proven.

## Operating rhythm

| Cadence | Decision focus |
| --- | --- |
| Daily | Delivery blockers, user/business feedback, support/moderation and key product health signals |
| Monday | Weekly priorities, owners, expected evidence and risks |
| Wednesday | Delivery/quality checkpoint; resolve blockers or reduce scope |
| Friday | Demo/review: shipped outcome, evidence learned, next decision |
| Monthly | Cohort/city/business health, budget/runway, roadmap gate and operating risk review |

Every item has an owner, intended outcome, metric/evidence, dependency, risk and next decision. Teams should surface bad news early; an unvalidated assumption is a discovery, not a failure to hide.

## Decision framework

Before accepting a feature, partnership or expansion ask:

1. Which user/business problem does it solve?
2. Does it strengthen the local network and existing modules?
3. Can success and harm be measured?
4. Are data, security, legal, operations and support requirements ready?
5. What is the smallest safe experiment or reversible release?

## First 12-month directional outcomes

Aim for a stable validated MVP, an initial city with meaningful active local supply and consumers, a working business/dashboard operating flow, a core team with clear ownership and evidence for first paid value. Specific counts, revenue and delivery dates are planning hypotheses to set with current market, budget and team data — not guaranteed outcomes.

## Final vision

Balkan.works is built through hundreds of disciplined, evidence-backed steps. Each step should make the local network more useful, trusted and ready for the next module.
